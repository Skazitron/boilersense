TCM 18000 - Exploring Intercultural technical Communication
Credit Hours: 3.00. This course will explore issues in diverse technical communication workplace settings using Intergroup Dialogue. Students will explore intercultural communication, conflict resolution, social identity, community, and social justice with diverse groups. A component of this class will be engaging with clients, co- facilitators, and/or guest speakers. Topics in this course will include a variety of social identities including (but not limited to) race/ethnicity, nationality of origin/citizenship, gender, sex, sexual orientation/attraction, SES/social class, age, religion/spirituality, ability/disability status, body size/type, level of education. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.
3.000 Credit hours

Syllabus Available
Levels: Graduate, Professional, Undergraduate
Schedule Types: Distance Learning, Lecture

Offered By: Regional Campus Only
Department: IUPUI Courses-IUPUI

Course Attributes:
Lower Division

May be offered at any of the following campuses:     
      IUPUI

Learning Outcomes: 1. Collaborate with others to build relationships in diverse groups. 2. Respond to derogatory comments and micro-agressions in the workplace. 3. Explore differences and commonalities between diverse groups. 4. Understand, define, use, and recognize each stage of the Intergroup Dialogue Four-Stage Model. 5. Engage in and promote empathy and equality in workplace settings.

Upper Division

May be offered at any of the following campuses:     
      IUPUI

Learning Outcomes: 1. Analyze examples of academic engineering, technical, and/or scientific written products and oral presentations to discover and identify conventions of discipline-specific content selection, organization, uses of visuals, and stylistic features. 2. Plan, prepare, and produce written and oral presentations, reports, and papers on engineering, technology, and science topics to technical and nontechnical audiences using the appropriate conventions for their fields. 3. Evaluate their own and other students' work, and provide constructive advice and feedback to colleagues as they develop their documents and presentations.

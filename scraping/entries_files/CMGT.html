CMGT 11000 - Introduction To Construction Management
Credit Hours: 3.00. An introduction to the construction management profession. Topics include: construction as a career; drafting standards; ethics; construction document reading and management; and Building Information Modeling (BIM) software.
0.000 OR 3.000 Credit hours

Syllabus Available
Levels: Graduate, Professional, Undergraduate
Schedule Types: Distance Learning, Laboratory, Lecture

Offered By: Regional Campus Only
Department: IUPUI Courses-IUPUI

Course Attributes:
Dept Credit, Lower Division

May be offered at any of the following campuses:     
      IUPUI

Learning Outcomes: 1. Interpret a set of standard construction drawings including but not limited to: Drawing types; Lines; Symbols; Abbreviations; Notation logic; Document set layout; and organization. 2. Utilize construction document related tools including: Architect's scale; Engineer's scale; Computer (drafting software). 3. Create a set of construction drawings using an industry standard software package (Revit). 4. Interact with a 3-dimensional building model utilizing BIM (building information modeling): Describe, define and interpret data derived from BIM; Manipulate digital information via BIM. 5. Analyze decisions in construction based on ethical principles. 6. Understand sustainable construction and related environmental issues.

CMGT 26000 - Statics
Credit Hours: 3.00. Forces acting on bodies at rest, including coplanar, concurrent, and non-concurring systems; equivalent force systems and distributed forces. Use of free body diagrams and equations of equilibrium in solving problems for array of determinate structural systems: trusses, pin frames, arches, cables, retaining walls, and beams. Load tracing to examine the overall structural conditions with regards to lateral and gravity loads.
3.000 Credit hours

Syllabus Available
Levels: Graduate, Professional, Undergraduate
Schedule Types: Distance Learning, Lecture

Offered By: Regional Campus Only
Department: IUPUI Courses-IUPUI

Course Attributes:
Lower Division

May be offered at any of the following campuses:     
      IUPUI

Learning Outcomes: 1. Analyze force systems acting at a single point. 2. Analyze force systems that are non-concurrent. 3. Analyze/design cable systems. 4. Analyze/design trusses by graphical and numerical methods. 5. Analyze/design pinned frames. 6. Analyze/design 3-hinged arches. 7. Trace the live and dead loads throughout a building structure.

CMGT 32000 - Scheduling And Project Control
Credit Hours: 3.00. A study of the planning and control of construction schedules. Topics include Gantt charts, critical path method (CPM) network scheduling, and monitoring and controlling the schedule. Lab work includes the application of MS Project for creating, presenting, revising, updating and controlling construction schedules.
3.000 Credit hours

Syllabus Available
Levels: Graduate, Professional, Undergraduate
Schedule Types: Distance Learning, Lecture

Offered By: Regional Campus Only
Department: IUPUI Courses-IUPUI

Course Attributes:
Upper Division

May be offered at any of the following campuses:     
      IUPUI

Learning Outcomes: 1. Describe each of the components of the "5 Step Process for Creating Realistic Project Schedules." 2. Use the techniques and methods presented in class to: draw project logic diagrams and schedules; calculate the times (ES, EF, LS, & LF); calculate floats; determine the critical path(s); explain and perform duration-cost trade-off; explain and perform project updating; explain and perform resource leveling; explain and perform the earned value approach; and use MS Project to create a project schedule including project controls.

CMGT 45000 - Structural Systems And Analysis
Credit Hours: 3.00. Analysis of the structural characteristics of common construction materials, including: wood, steel, concrete, masonry, and light gauge metal in order to integrate structural elements into complete structural systems in the construction of modern buildings and structures to withstand gravity, wind, earthquake, and other environmental forces.
3.000 Credit hours

Syllabus Available
Levels: Graduate, Professional, Undergraduate
Schedule Types: Distance Learning, Lecture

Offered By: Regional Campus Only
Department: IUPUI Courses-IUPUI

Course Attributes:
Credit By Exam, Upper Division

May be offered at any of the following campuses:     
      IUPUI

Learning Outcomes: 1. Define the engineering properties of reinforced concrete, wood and steel. 2. Interpret building code information and apply them to the analysis and design of simple structural components such as beams, columns, slabs, walls and foundations as it relates to wood, steel and concrete. 3.

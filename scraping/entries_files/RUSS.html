RUSS 34100 - Russian Literature In The Nineteenth Century

Increase ability to write analytical papers supporting a thesis. 6. Increase ability to conduct and present independent research. 7. Confront the philosophical and ethical questions raised by these works.

RUSS 38000 - Russian Culture And Civilization I
Credit Hours: 3.00. This course traces the intellectual and cultural development of Russia, starting with the founding of the Kievan state and continuing to the last days of the Russian Empire. No knowledge of Russian is required. Typically offered Fall, Spring.
3.000 Credit hours

Syllabus Available
Levels: Graduate, Professional, Undergraduate
Schedule Types: Distance Learning, Lecture

Offered By: College of Liberal Arts
Department: School of Languages & Cultures

Course Attributes:
Upper Division

May be offered at any of the following campuses:     
      West Lafayette

Learning Outcomes: 1. Understand the forces and circumstances (geographical, historical, political) that shaped the Russian state and gave rise to its distinctive forms of culture. 2. Understand the distinction between rossiskoe (Russian as pertains to the polity) and russkoe (ethnically and linguistically Russian), and improve understanding of Russia as a multi-ethnic state. 3. Discover the contributions of non-ethnic Russians to Russian culture. 4. Develop an appreciation for the artistic value of examples of Russian literature, music, and the pictorial arts. 5. Increase ability to read critically and write persuasively.


RUSS 38100 - Russian Culture And Civilization II
Credit Hours: 3.00. This course traces the intellectual and cultural development of Russia from the revolutionary period to the present. No knowledge of Russian is required. Typically offered Fall, Spring.
3.000 Credit hours

Syllabus Available
Levels: Graduate, Professional, Undergraduate
Schedule Types: Distance Learning, Lecture

Offered By: College of Liberal Arts
Department: School of Languages & Cultures

Course Attributes:
Upper Division

May be offered at any of the following campuses:     
      West Lafayette

Learning Outcomes: 1. Increase understanding of the interactions between revolutionary politics and revolutionary art, and explore the role of art in a political society while encountering various strategies authors, composers, etc. used to navigate between creative impulses and political requirements. 2. Move beyond the simplistic dichotomy of propaganda vs. dissidence and explore the complex gradations between the extremes of official anti-Soviet culture. 3. Increase knowledge of the relationship between cultural production, distribution, and consumption and the forms that culture takes. 4. Develop an appreciation for the artistic value of examples of Russian literature, music, and the pictorial arts.

DHYG H2420 - Introduction To Dentistry
Credit Hours: 1.00 to 3.00. An overview of the field of dentistry with emphasis on the specialties in dentistry, and introduction to common dental procedures with information a hygienist needs in patient education, as well as issues related to access to dental care and the dental workforce.
1.000 TO 3.000 Credit hours

Syllabus Available
Levels: Graduate, Professional, Undergraduate
Schedule Types: Distance Learning, Lecture

Offered By: Regional Campus Only

Course Attributes:
Lower Division

May be offered at any of the following campuses:     
      IU Fort Wayne

Learning Outcomes: 1. List and describe issues facing our nation related to dental care. 2. Describe steps that can be taken to improve oral health in America. 3. Describe the ADHA’s outlook on the profession of dental hygiene. 4. Describe ADA’s outlook on the future of dentistry. 5. List and describe the specialties of dentistry, as well as the educational requirements for each specialty. 6. Describe procedures performed by each specialist within the dental profession. 7. Discuss commonly asked questions within the dental profession which patients might ask of a dental professional. 8. Define or recognize general terminology associated with each specialty. 9. Demonstrate the use of traditional and electronic resources in support of evidence-based inquiry/research strategies. 10. Demonstrate knowledge of the laws and regulations related to dental hygiene practice in the state of Indiana. 11. Demonstrate understanding of plagiarism and the ramifications of its use in writing. 12. Develop critical thinking skills in analyzing an article utilizing a template. 13. Demonstrate knowledge of vocabulary terminology related to the dental profession.

DHYG H3030 - Radiology-Second Year
Credit Hours: 1.00 to 3.00. Principles associated with production of x-rays and manipulation of x-ray equipment.
0.000 TO 3.000 Credit hours

Syllabus Available
Levels: Graduate, Professional, Undergraduate
Schedule Types: Distance Learning, Laboratory, Lecture

Offered By: Regional Campus Only

Course Attributes:
Upper Division

May be offered at any of the following campuses:     
      IU Fort Wayne

Learning Outcomes: 1. Discuss the generation of x-ray energy and how x-rays interact with matter. 2. Explain the function of x-ray machine exposure factors, how they interrelate and how they affect the receptor. 3. Discuss the type, purpose and content of typical intraoral and extraoral dental radiographs. 4. Compare and contrast the paralleling and the bisecting angle intraoral techniques. 5. Describe the clinical procedures involved in intraoral paralleling periapical and bitewing techniques. 6. Identify and correct technical and processing errors on intraoral radiographs. 7. Explain the purpose of and perform effective radiographic infection control measures. 8. Describe and outline film processing and quality assurance procedures. 9. Discuss and apply the principles of radiation biology, selection criteria, radiation safety and protection in dental radiography. 10. Recognize the characteristic radiographic features of anatomic landmarks, tooth structure and dental materials, carious lesions, periodontal disease and periapical pathology on dental radiographs. 11. Correctly arrange, mount and label finished radiographs. 12. Describe the principles and procedures involved in the duplication of radiographs.

FVS 26100 - Foundations Of Cinema Production
Credit Hours: 3.00. The course introduces students to the principles and practice of professional video production. Through lectures, viewing, discussions, and laboratory exercises, students will acquire the tools necessary to transform their concepts, or those of their clients, into successful video productions. Film students have priority. Typically offered Fall Spring.
0.000 OR 3.000 Credit hours

Syllabus Available
Levels: Graduate, Professional, Undergraduate
Schedule Types: Distance Learning, Individual Study, Laboratory, Lecture

Offered By: College of Liberal Arts
Department: School of Interdis Studies

Course Attributes:
Credit By Exam, Lower Division

May be offered at any of the following campuses:     
      West Lafayette

Learning Outcomes: 1. Provide an introduction to the cinema production cycle. 2. Involve the student in preparing materials specific to each stage of the cinema production cycle. 3. Summarize visual storytelling concepts and production terminology. 4. Define concepts of cinema production and image capture techniques. 5. Introduce students to industry specific copyright laws and regulations. 6. Introduce students to career opportunities including narrative, documentary, television and live event production.

FVS 33000 - International Cinema
Credit Hours: 3.00. This course is designed to develop students seeing, reading, speaking, writing, and critical thinking skills and especially to improve visual competency, awareness for visual culture, and knowledge of selected international cinema. All films will be shown with English subtitles. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.
0.000 OR 3.000 Credit hours

Syllabus Available
Levels: Graduate, Professional, Undergraduate
Schedule Types: Distance Learning, Laboratory, Lecture

Offered By: College of Liberal Arts
Department: School of Interdis Studies

Course Attributes:
Upper Division

May be offered at any of the following campuses:     
      West Lafayette

Learning Outcomes: 1. Explain and assess different ways of seeing as an introduction to understanding visual theory. 2. Demonstrate understanding of the concepts and techniques of cinematography, processing, and reading images and film. 3. Recognize, select, and justify choices for seeing and interpreting the visual. 4. Identify and analyze culturally-conditioned references.

FVS 49900 - Capstone In Film/Video/Media Production
Credit Hours: 2.00 or 4.00. The Capstone in Film/Video/Media Production is intended to be the culminating experience of the Film/Video Studies or Film/Video and Theatre Production major. Each student Students will create a project of their own design. Students will pitch their project ideas to the program director and/or faculty and should develop a project based around their future career goals. Example areas include narrative/fiction cinema, documentary/non-fiction filmmaking, television production, live event production, etc. Students will fulfill the role of Writer/Producer/Director for their project (and potentially Director of Photography, Editor, etc.). This creative work will culminate in a presentation to an audience. Permission of instructor required. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.
2.000 OR 4.000 Credit hours

Syllabus Available
Levels: Graduate, Professional, Undergraduate
Schedule Types: Individual Study

Offered By: College of Liberal Arts
Department: School of Interdis Studies

Course Attributes:
Upper Division

May be offered at any of the following campuses:     
      West Lafayette

Learning Outcomes: 1. Develop a complete and significant project idea/vision/creative work. 2. Plan out the execution of the creative work. 3. Assemble a team with which to craft the developed vision. 4. Complete the work to a professional level of quality. 5. Present the work to an audience with a Q&A session.

AFT 11000 - Heritage And Values I
Credit Hours: 1.00. A survey course designed to introduce students to the United States Air and Space Forces and provides an overview of the basic characteristics, missions, and organization of the Air and Space Forces.
1.000 Credit hours

Syllabus Available
Levels: Graduate, Professional, Undergraduate
Schedule Types: Distance Learning, Lecture

Offered By: Polytechnic Institute
Department: Aerospace Studies

Course Attributes:
Credit By Exam, Dept Credit, Lower Division, Military

May be offered at any of the following campuses:     
      West Lafayette

Learning Outcomes: 1. Describe general aspects of the Department of the Air Force. 2. Describe leadership fundamentals for Air Force Officers. 3. Identify service benefits and opportunities for officers serving in the Air Force. 4. Explain the history and evolution of the United States Air Force and Space Force. 5. Demonstrate fundamental written and verbal communication skills.

AFT 20220 - Air Force ROTC Leadership Laboratory
Credit Hours: 1.00.  Leadership Laboratory (LLAB) is a dynamic and integrated grouping of leadership developmental activities designed to meet the needs and expectations of prospective Department of the Air Force second lieutenants and to complement the AFROTC academic program. It is a student planned, organized, and executed practicum conducted under the supervision of the Detachment Commander and Operations Flight Commander.
1.000 Credit hours

Syllabus Available
Levels: Graduate, Professional, Undergraduate
Schedule Types: Distance Learning, Laboratory

Offered By: Polytechnic Institute
Department: Division of Military Science

Course Attributes:
Lower Division

May be offered at any of the following campuses:     
      West Lafayette

Repeatable for Additional Credit: Yes - May be repeated for a maximum of 4 credits

Learning Outcomes: 1. Explain and practice the Air Force Core Values - Integrity First, Service before Self, and Excellence in All We Do. 2. Discuss characteristics of the Air/Space Force way of life. 3. Practice basic leadership, followership, and teamwork skills. 4. Demonstrate the mental and physical skills necessary to succeed in the AFROTC Field Training Environment. 5. Apply intermediate leadership, followership, and teamwork skills.

AFT 35100 - Leading People And Effective Communication I
Credit Hours: 3.00. Leading People and Effective Communication utilizes student’s field training experience to take a more in-depth look at leadership. Special emphasis is placed on enhancing communication skills, and why that is important as a leader. Students have an opportunity to try out these leadership and management techniques in a supervised environment as juniors and seniors.
3.000 Credit hours

Syllabus Available
Levels: Graduate, Professional, Undergraduate
Schedule Types: Distance Learning, Lecture

Offered By: Polytechnic Institute
Department: Aerospace Studies

Course Attributes:
Credit By Exam, Dept Credit, Military, Upper Division

May be offered at any of the following campuses:     
      West Lafayette

Learning Outcomes: 1. Compare and contrast individual leadership skills and approaches. 2. Analyze personal leadership strengths and weaknesses. 3. Explain the responsibility and authority of an Air Force officer. 4. Explain Air Force officer’s duties and responsibilities as a subordinate leader. 5. Practice the Air Force officer’s responsibilities in the counseling and feedback process. 6. Evaluate and apply concepts of ethical behavior. 7. Evaluate and analyze concepts, principles, and theories of quality in Air Force leadership and management. 8. Demonstrate listening, speaking, and writing skills in Air Force-specific formats.

AFT 47100 - National Security/Commissioning Preparation I
Credit Hours: 3.00. National Security/Commissioning Preparation is designed for college seniors and gives them the foundation to understand their role as military officers and how they are directly tied to our National Security Strategy. It is an overview of the complex social and political issues facing the military profession and requires a measure of sophistication commensurate with the senior college level.
3.000 Credit hours

Syllabus Available
Levels: Graduate, Professional, Undergraduate
Schedule Types: Distance Learning, Lecture

Offered By: Polytechnic Institute
Department: Aerospace Studies

Course Attributes:
Credit By Exam, Dept Credit, Military, Upper Division

May be offered at any of the following campuses:     
      West Lafayette

Learning Outcomes: 1. Describe the basic elements of national security policy and process. 2. Identify the basic Air Force and Space Force warfighting domains. 3. Describe the roles of the military in society. 4. Discuss current domestic and international issues affecting the military profession. 5. Describe fundamental provisions of the military justice system. 6. Describe the responsibility, authority, and functions of an Air Force commander. 7. Demonstrate listening, speaking, and writing skills in Air Force-specific formats. 8. Plan for life as a second lieutenant.

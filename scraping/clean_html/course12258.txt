EDCI 66000 - Learning Design And Technology Seminar

Credit Hours: 1.00.  Investigation of trends, developments, and issues in educational technology. Focus is on current practices, problems, and research. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Familiarize self with resources available to support progression through the LDT Master's program.
2.  Learn the specifics of the LDT Master's program and its requirements, including reviewing targeted learning competencies, portfolio requirements, program faculty, and the graduate handbook. You will also create a draft of your personal plan of study.
3.  Examine skills and approaches to learning critical to your success as a student in the online program and expected of all program students, including effective time management, avoiding plagiarism, utilizing proper online etiquette and incorporating a positive disposition in your interactions with peer students, faculty and staff.
4.  Identify a career pathway that is appropriate for you including the skills, knowledge, and attitudes you will need to be successful.
5.  Apply group project skills and management techniques to make you successful in future courses and your career.
6.  Discuss the milestones of the LDT program, including the practicum and portfolio experience and the program competencies.

PUBH 10001 - Introduction To Public Health

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course serves as an introduction to the field of public health. Topics include identification of the principal determinants of health and how they affect individuals and populations, the role of government and public health professionals in health and healthcare, and critical and emerging issues in public health in the US and around the world.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Acquire a basic understanding of the field of Public Health.
2.  Identify the principal determinants of health.
3.  Explain the role of public health in society.
4.  Understand the government's role in matters pertaining to the health of the public.
5.  Understand the role of health disparities in public health.
6.  Achieve a basic understanding of the major investigative tool in public health: Epidemiology.
7.  Identify the principal environmental determinants of health.
8.  Know major and widespread challenges to good health: smoking, obesity, chronic disease, violence, and social and economic inequities.
9.  Recognize critical and emerging issues in global health, including the current pandemic.

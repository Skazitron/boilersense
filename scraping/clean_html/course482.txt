ENGL 60611 - Technical Communication

Credit Hours: 3.00.  The purpose of this course is to show students how to write technical documentation and produce high-quality and reader-centered documents. They learn to write documents in several modalities, including print, electronic, audio, and video. Permission of department required. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Apply technical concepts, processes, and terms effectively for the purposes of informing and persuading.
2.  Identify, analyze, and understand the particular written genres and conventions of various workplaces so that they can be shaped to fit one's professional needs. 
3.  Classify and identify audiences for specific technical documents. 
4.  Demonstrate an understanding audience needs by crafting writing, designing documents, and producing audio-visual texts that address and take those needs into consideration.
5.  Produce and manage common professional documents in collaboration with others.

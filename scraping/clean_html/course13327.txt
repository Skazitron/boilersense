DANC 34600 - Intermediate Choreography

Credit Hours: 1.00 or 2.00.  A study of selected principles underlying the design of group performance in modern dance. Permission of instructor required.

    1.000 OR     2.000 Credit hours


Syllabus Available

Levels: Graduate, Professional, Undergraduate 

Schedule Types: Distance Learning, Individual Study, Studio

May be offered at any of the following campuses:      

      West Lafayette


Repeatable for Additional Credit: Yes - May be repeated for a maximum of 6 credits


Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Use skills learned in DANC 34500 in a creative process for mainstage dance work.
2.  Collaborate with dancers, lighting designers, costume designers, stage technicians, sound designers and all dance faculty.  
3.  Learn leadership skills by becoming models of responsibility: demonstrating proactive behavior by serving on the Purdue Contemporary Dance Company (PCDC), student committee, assisting PCDC officers and volunteering to assist with other Dance Division concerts.




Prerequisites: 

 Undergraduate level  DANC 34500 Minimum Grade of D-

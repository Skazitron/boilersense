CPB 63600 - Veterinary Microbiology Seminar

Credit Hours: 0.00 to 8.00.  This class will provide graduate students with in-depth knowledge in current microbiology research and clinical diagnostics. Students will review current literature related to veterinary infectious disease with the intent of explaining its application to clinical and diagnostic microbiology. DVM or equivalent required and/or currently enrolled in Ph.D. graduate program in an animal related discipline. Permission of instructor required.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Able to explain and apply current infectious disease/diagnostic literature.
2.  Able to critically evaluate all aspects of diagnostic cases: clinical signs, sample collection, sample transport, test selection, test methods, result interpretation, and application.

CLCS 23700 - Gender And Sexuality In Greek And Roman Antiquity

Credit Hours: 3.00.  How identities based on gender, sexual behavior and sexual desire, and socio-economic status are established in ancient Greece and Rome. Exploration of why these ancient views of gender and sexuality remain of continuing importance in the 21st century. All readings in English.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Learn core information about ancient Greek and/or Roman cultures.
2.  Learn to analyze gender identities and conventions surrounding sexuality in various communities with different socio-political structures.
3.  Develop methods for historically sensitive investigation of materials (literary and/or visual) from ancient cultures.
4.  Systematically improve analytical skills as writers, readers, and speakers.

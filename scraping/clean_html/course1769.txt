BCM 46200 - Residential Design Build

Credit Hours: 2.00.  A study of the design/build project delivery system for residential construction, including home design fundamentals, construction methods, disability code requirements, building code requirements. Course content includes partial fulfillment of the requirements for the National Association of Home Builders, Certified Aging-In-Place (CAPS) designation.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Describe concepts relating to Universal Design/Build and Aging in Place when designing residential construction.
2.  Describe specific design solutions for various client requirements including but not limited to ADA, Aging in Place, etc.
3.  Identify special considerations for estimating and scheduling a design/build project.
4.  Identify considerations and recommendations for executing the job while the client is in residence.
5.  Discuss common Design Build business challenges and learn practical tips and tools to overcome them.

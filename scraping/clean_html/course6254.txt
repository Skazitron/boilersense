VCS 86004 - Large Animal After-Hours & Emergency Rotation

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course is designed to provide experience in the principles of intensive medical care and management of large animal emergency cases. Students will work with the attending clinician(s) and nursing staff to care for the emergency and critical care cases admitted to the hospital. As emergency cases are unscheduled and their timing is unpredictable, there will be both in-hospital shifts as well as times that students are on call during this rotation. (129 clinical hours). Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Assess and develop a diagnostic and/or treatment plan for emergency large animal patients in a timely manner reflecting the urgency of the particular case.
2.  Interpret diagnostic tests and utilize them to make clinical decisions in the ongoing management of emergency large animal patients.
3.  Utilize clinical monitoring modalities associated with emergency and critical case cases in large animals to assess and correctly identify changes in a patients' clinical status.
4.  Perform competently entry-level clinical procedures on large animal emergency and critical care patients.
5.  Work effectively as part of a team in delivering emergency health care to large animal patients, including client and team communications, case triage and patient transfer.
6.  Complete an accurate medical record, including history, physical examination, progress notes and surgical reports (when applicable) for emergency large animal patients.

AT 21900 - Unmanned Aerial Systems Design, Build, Test

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course will further develop skills, and understanding of small aerial systems. Emphasis will be on design, selection, construction, flight test, evaluation, and repair, of stabilized unmanned aircraft. Emphasis will be placed on advancing manual flight control skills utilizing an advanced simulator and student constructed rotorcraft.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Identify correct powerplants for rotorcraft.
2.  Demonstrate weight and balance measurement and calculation methods.
3.  Demonstrate programming of manual flight transmitters.
4.  Demonstrate programming of flight stabilization systems.
5.  Demonstrate safety procedures during operations.
6.  Construct and correctly install components of flight control and power systems.

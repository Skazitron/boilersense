PHRM 46600 - Pharmaceutical Care In Oncology

Credit Hours: 1.00.  This course aims to expand students' understanding of hematology and oncology topics. After completion, the student should be able to apply course concepts to actual patient cases.  CLPH 49000 will be collaboratively taught by oncology pharmacists and residents from the greater Indianapolis area hospitals. Students will be exposed to relevant topics including current events, supportive care, bone marrow transplant, renal cell carcinoma, specialty pharmacy administration and leadership, oral chemotherapy, palliative care and career paths in oncology pharmacy. Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Demonstrate critical thinking and decision making assessed through current event discussions and patient case presentations.
2.  Demonstrate communication skills and abilities assessed through current event discussions and mock patient counseling experience.
3.  Demonstrate information literacy assessed through current event discussions.
4.  Demonstrate professionalism and ethics assessed through current event discussions and palliative care case/discussions.
5.  Demonstrate competence and social awareness assessed throughout all discussions and projects as it pertains to individuals with the diagnosis of cancer.

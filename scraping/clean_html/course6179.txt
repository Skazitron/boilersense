VCS 80201 - Professional Skills I

Credit Hours: 1.00.  This course builds on the skills taught in VCS 80100 and VCS 80200 Behavior, Husbandry and Diagnostic Techniques I and II. Students will be taught additional clinical skills and practice them in the clinical skills laboratory. Communication skills will be taught with the opportunity to practice them in role-plays. Financial management, professionalism and wellness topics will also be discussed. Students will participate in the care of teaching animals. Typically offered Fall.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Demonstrate the core communication skills and apply them in a client encounter.
2.  Take a thorough and accurate history and record it correctly in the medical record.
3.  Perform a complete physical examination and detect major abnormalities.
4.  Record the physical examination findings in the medical record.
5.  Perform orthopedic examinations in dogs and lameness exams in horses and cattle.
6.  Describe the differences between a lameness exam in large animals and small animals.
7.  Correctly restrain and examine pocket pets, pet birds and poultry.
8.  Apply bandages to the limbs of small animals.
9.  Understand their educational budget and the impact of educational loan borrowing.
10.  Discuss strategies for stress management and recognize what works for them.

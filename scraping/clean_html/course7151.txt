HTM 61200 - HTM Management Challenge Analysis

Credit Hours: 3.00.  A course designed to discuss specific management problems in the hospitality and tourism industries. Through the use of case studies, students study current challenges, management analysis, the process of decision making, and the impacts of corporate decisions. Prerequisite: basic management organization class. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Gain exposure and knowledge of strategic management topics in preparation for graduate coursework, applied projects, and future research.
2.  Critically consider and assess the merits and weaknesses of specific strategic management concepts and theories.
3.  Develop analytical skills through tasks such as critical analysis and discussion of cases.

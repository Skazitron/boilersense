PTEC 40802 - Capstone And Planning II

Credit Hours:  3.00. Part two of the final in a series of transdisciplinary, real-world, project-based work and conversations exploring the intersections of humanities and technology, social and natural sciences. Capstone projects emphasize design thinking; collaboration; communication; hands-on prototyping; testing; assessing. Learners will engage in individual and team project work and conversations that will competency proficiency based on the learner's experience, goals, and learning needs.  Typically offered Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1. Demonstrate an emerging to proficient level of understanding of issues related to the intersection of science, technology, and quantitative reasoning using project-based techniques to address unstructured, real-world problems.
2. Demonstrate an emerging to proficient level of understanding and skill by completing an authentic, full-lifecycle individual or team based project.
3. Demonstrate a proficient level of understanding of connecting acquired knowledge and skills to the primary program competencies.

OBHR 60500 - Research Methods I

Credit Hours: 3.00.  An introduction to research methods. This seminar examines the use of research methods to develop empirical tests for theoretical and practical issues. It includes readings and discussions on the philosophy of science, reliability, validity, research design, threats to research, ethics, and other issues in research methodology. Students formulate hypotheses and an original research design to test their hypotheses.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Identify key decisions organizational researchers must make when crafting research proposals and conducting studies.  
2.  Demonstrate ability to generate a sound research proposal, based on course concepts and key research decisions discussed throughout the course.
3.  Criticize the research methods employed in prior published works in the organizational literature, comparing and contrasting exemplary versus less-than-exemplary approaches.

AT 65900 - Airport And Transportation Sustainability

Credit Hours: 3.00.  (AT 65900) Airport and Transportation Sustainability is a doctoral format course about the methods and practice of sustainability for airports and transportation. Sustainability in planning, design, construction and operation of airports and transportation facilities and infrastructure are addressed. Prerequisites: Successful completion of at least one statistics course covering descriptive and inferential statistics, an airport management course (e.g., AT 35900) and admission to doctoral program. Permission of instructor required.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Critically review, synthesize, document and present relevant research literature.
2.  Identify a problem statement in airport or transportation facility sustainability and develop a research proposal to address the problem.
3.  Work effectively and collaboratively as part of a team.
4.  Execute high quality research, document findings in a research paper and communicate key principles regarding their research in a professional presentation.
5.  Independently motivated, able to work with self-direction, and capable of thoughtful and analytical discussion of relevant topics in sustainability as applied to airports and transportation facilities.

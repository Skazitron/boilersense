NS 33000 - Evolution Of Warfare

Credit Hours: 3.00. The purpose of Evolution of Warfare is to imbue students with an understanding of the origins of modern warfighting theory through an analysis of historical philosophies, domains, tactics and technology.  Through a study of military history, students explore the timeless fundamentals of armed conflict as well as the central role of innovation.  They will apply these fundamental warfighting concepts to historic amphibious and expeditionary operations through guided analysis and decision games.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  State the major cultural, technological, organizational, and doctrinal transformations in warfare.
2.  State the operational history of amphibious and expeditionary warfare.
3.  Recite the warrior ethos.
4.  Describe the relationship between the tactical, operational, and strategic level of warfare.
5.  Describe the origins of current USMC and Joint doctrine, especially MCDP 1 and MCDP 1-0.
6.  Apply symmetric and asymmetric principles across all warfighting domains.
7.  Apply knowledge from historical conflicts to modern and future challenges.
8.  Demonstrate fundamental writing and speaking skills required of a marine officer.
9.  Demonstrate a tactical mindset and effective decision-making.

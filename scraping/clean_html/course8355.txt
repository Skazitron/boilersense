ANTH 60600 - Quantitative Research Design

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Focuses on quantitative research design, methodology, and ethics, discussing the appropriate selection of any methods. Students gain experience in generating and analyzing of quantitative and mixed-method data, and in the writing up and communication of quantitative research, altogether aiming for statistical theory.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Meet the Graduate School learning objectives of knowledge, communication, critical thinking, and ethical research through the goals listed here.
2.  Learn about quantitative research design, ethics and methods.
3.  Understand when and how to appropriately select qualitative and quantitative methods and identify how and when to use them.
4.  Gain an understanding on how anthropologists across the subfields use and other scholars apply quantitative methods.
5.  Gain experience in generating and analyzing quantitative data, including being versed in common statistical packages (such as SPSS).
6.  Achieve statistical literacy for understanding and working in a mixed methods environment.

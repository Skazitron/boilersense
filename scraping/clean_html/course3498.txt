COM 41600 - United States Politics And The Media

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course examines the roles and influences of the mass media on American politics generally and with particular emphasis on election campaigns and the evolving political culture of the U.S.

    3.000 Credit hours

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Understand how media influence perceptions of political issues and events.
2.  Recognize the importance of media in the formation and maintenance of individual attitudes and public opinion.
3.  Able to create and analyze political messages in the context of campaigns.
4.  Understand gender, race, ethnicity, sexual orientation, and other forms of diversity in relation to mass and political communications.
5.  Able to write correctly and clearly in forms and styles appropriate for communications professions and audiences.

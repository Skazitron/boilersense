AD 38200 - A Global History Of Modern Art

Credit Hours: 3.00.  A study of artistic circulations and cultural changes between Europe, Asia, Africa, and the Middle East from the 18th century, designed to examine the history of modern art in a global and critical way.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Identify and explain historical events, social forces, and cultural trends that have had an influence on these artworks, artists, and movements.
2.  Compare and contrast them by perceiving and reflecting on their differences and similarities.
3.  Interpret statements made about global artistic circulations by contextualizing and analyzing them and selecting artworks to illustrate their comments.
4.  Apply knowledge acquired to analyze and write about artworks not studies in class.
5.  Build knowledge and understanding of global art and develop analytical and written skills.

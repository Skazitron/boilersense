HDFS 20100 - Introduction To Family Processes

Credit Hours: 3.00.  An examination of the interpersonal processes that take place within family contexts. Emphasis is on family dynamics. Also considered are linkages between family processes and the broader social environment. Basic components of the research process will be introduced. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Apply family theories to the unique interactions and dynamics that occur within a family.
2.  Understand diversity in family structure in our society.
3.  Identify family interaction patterns.
4.  Understand and identify the importance of family research.

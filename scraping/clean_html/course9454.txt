FNR 24250 - Laboratory In Ecology And Systematics Of Fishes, Amphibians And Reptiles

Credit Hours: 1.00.  Basic anatomy, classification, and identification of fishes, amphibians and reptiles. Identification deals with representative species from selected phylogenetic and geographic groupings in North American. Typically offered Fall.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Learn to identify important North American fish, amphibians, and reptile species, with an emphasis on species of the Midwestern U.S.
2.  Be familar with the ecology, natural history, and systematics of the fish, amphibians, and reptiles.
3.  Describe the characteristic features of some of the important North American vertebrates, using a combination of preserved specimens, photographic images, field guides, and taxomonic keys.

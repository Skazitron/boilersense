CGT 33500 - Game Scripting

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Students gain a deeper understanding into video game scripting. Utilizing a common project, students use scripted objects to customize and enhance a small existing game. While the projects are individualized for the student, they are trained to utilize best practices for team based, industry applications of their work. Permission of department required.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Create reusable game objects that perform useful functions in standard video game genres.
2.  Reinforce proper techniques in scripting.
3.  Achieve mastery of Unreal blueprint scripting and visual scripting generally.
4.  Learn how to drive gameplay with imported data.
5.  Achieve a rudimentary baseline of C++ scripting.
6.  Become familiar with Visual Studio.

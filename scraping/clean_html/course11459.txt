PSY 30500 - Understanding And Analyzing Psychological Data

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course is an upper level undergraduate statistics course for students in psychology and related fields who are interested in conducting or understanding quantitative research. Students should have successfully completed an introductory statistics course (e.g., PSY 20100). The course will begin with an accelerated review of fundamentals concepts (e.g., data visualization, central limit theorem, probability) and then will focus primarily on regression. Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Demonstrate advanced conceptual understanding of foundational statistical principles.
2.  Demonstrate ability to correctly analyze data by hand and using SPSS.
3.  Demonstrate skills in managing their own data.
4.  Demonstrate the ability to comprehensively analyze and interpret the results of correlation, simple regression, multiple regression, and two-way interaction models.
5.  Demonstrate proper interpretation.
6.  Demonstrate the ability to accurately report results of statistical analyses in APA format.

EDCI 55100 - Young Adult Literature

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course is a survey of classic and contemporary young adult texts. Students are introduced to the genre through reading notable young adult texts, applying relevant literary criticism, and investigating effective pedagogical strategies.  Typically offered Fall Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Read and respond to selected young adult texts in writing and in class discussion.
2.  Explore the extent to which young adult texts portray the complex challenges of teenage life.
3.  Explore the extent to which young adult texts, as imaginative narratives, affect teenage identity development.
4.  Create a final project synthesizing young adult texts, theories of reading and response, and theories of adolescent development.

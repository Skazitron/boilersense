HK 41500 - Exercise As Medicine

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Provide students with the concepts, techniques, and skills to investigate the scientific evidence on physical activity and health; and to design and implement interventions to make physical activity a part of daily campus culture. Permission of instructor required. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

    3.000 Credit hours


Syllabus Available

Levels: Graduate, Professional, Undergraduate 

Schedule Types: Distance Learning, Lecture 


Offered By: College of Health & Human Sci 

Department: Dept of Health & Kinesiology 


Course Attributes: Upper Division 


May be offered at any of the following campuses:      

      West Lafayette


Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Recognize and describe the consequences of regular physical activity (PA) and inactivity on individual and community health.
2.  Recognize and describe the causes of and solutions for health disparities in communities.
3.  Develop activities to promote physical activity on campus.
4.  Develop activities to promote physical activity through social media and online content.
5.  Evaluate the success of activities promoting physical activity.




Restrictions:

HTM 64200 - Advanced Personnel Systems In Restaurants, Hotels, And Institutions

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Examination of the philosophical and operational alternatives related to establishing and directing human resource systems in complex restaurant, hotel, and institutional organizations. Prerequisite:  HTM 34100, 41100; 6 credit hours at the upper division undergraduate level in Business, Management, Marketing. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  List leading issues impacting human resource management in hospitality businesses nationally and globally.
2.  Suggest appropriate business responses to those HR issues.
3.  Explain the role of the human resource office at the corporate level in multi-unit hospitality companies.
4.  Craft strategies that would successfully recruit, select, train, develop, compensate and manage the performance of assignees working in multi-unit hospitality businesses.
5.  Develop strategies that would sustain a hospitality business' corporate culture across national and international borders.
6.  Describe ways used by large multi-unit hospitality companies to effectively communicate and engage its subsidiaries.

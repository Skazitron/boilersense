RUSS 34200 - Revolution, Repression, Renewal: Soviet Literature And Beyond

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Selected Russian poetry, prose, and drama of the 20th century in historical and cultural context. Students will read and discuss major authors and texts and will write critical essays on literary topics. Conducted largely in Russian. Typically offered Fall Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Understand the role and position of literature in the Soviet Union.
2.  Situate the works they read in their historical and political context.
3.  Understand the relationship between writers, the Soviet state, and the means of disseminating texts.
4.  Improve understanding of the way literary works function through various artistic devices.
5.  Improve ability to perform close readings of literary texts.
6.  Increase ability to write analytical papers supporting a thesis.
7.  Increase ability to conduct and present independent research.
8.  Confront the philosophical and ethical questions raised by these works.

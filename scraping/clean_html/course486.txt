ENGL 60900 - Seminar In Fiction Writing

Credit Hours: 3.00.  An advanced course in the writing of fiction. Workshop critiques. Prerequisite:  Admission to the creative writing program. Typically offered Fall Spring.

    3.000 Credit hours


Syllabus Available

Levels: Graduate, Professional, Undergraduate 

Schedule Types: Lecture 


Offered By: College of Liberal Arts 

Department: English 



May be offered at any of the following campuses:      

      West Lafayette


Repeatable for Additional Credit: Yes - May be repeated an unlimited number of times


Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Enhance reading skills through close examination of other writers, including classmates and professional writers.
2.  Enhance vocabulary for the creative writing discourse community.
3.  Enhance feedback skills through thoughtful, frank, tactful, and intelligent discussion of your classmates stories and novel chapters.
4.  Expand reading and writing aesthetics, and understanding of what literature is and can be, that will guide your reading and writing in the future.
5.  Produce fiction benefitting a third-year thesis.

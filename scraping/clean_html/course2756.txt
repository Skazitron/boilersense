TLI 62579 - Global, Legal And Ethical Issues In Technology Leadership

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course examines the global, legal and ethical issues that influence global technology leadership and management. Topics include ethical decision making; management; global issues; intellectual property and innovation; business law; relevant international laws, public policies and regulations, risk management, strategic alliances and joint ventures, and issues of organizational sustainability. This course relies on the case-study approach and covers a broad range of current and emerging technologies and businesses.  Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Assess the pros and cons of various perspectives and approaches to global technology leadership and innovation management.
2.  Discuss and debate ethical and legal issues that influence, affect or drive technology leadership, operations, innovation and sustainability.
3.  Analyze the ethical and legal issues in case studies by applying specific methods of case analysis and decision making.
4.  Engage in reflective practice applying course content to real life cases and practical work situations.
5.  Identify issues and apply strategies related to international joint ventures.
6.  Demonstrate an in depth understanding of policies and regulations related to technology management.

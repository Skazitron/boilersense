VCS 88002 - Clinical Veterinary Services For The Indiana State Fair

Credit Hours: 3.00.  The course is a 3-credit clinical rotation. A PVM clinician will lead this elective 3-week clinical rotation. Students will spend 3 weeks providing veterinary care to all exhibition animals at the Indiana State Fair. The veterinary services provided include health assessment during check-in, assessment of ill animals, and on-call service after business hours. The majority of cases will be swine and cattle, but can also include horses, poultry, and small ruminants. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Increase the student's clinical experience in diagnosis and treatment of common disease conditions in a wide variety of species.
2.  Develop communication skills in an environment which presents diverse interactions (ex. client, general public).
3.  Develop leadership skills.
4.  Develop an understanding of regulatory medicine, to include animal welfare, for livestock exhibition.
5.  Increase the student's exposure to disease biosecurity and control.

PSY 67400 - Structural Equation Modeling

Credit Hours: 3.00.   This is an advanced course in structural equation modeling (SEM), intended to provide doctoral students with an introductory treatment of the theory and methods of SEM. SEM is a statistical methodology that encompasses a wide variety of models, including path models, exploratory and confirmatory factor models, structural regression models, and latent growth models, among others. Prerequisites: PSY 61000 is highly recommended but not required. Permission of department required. Typically offered Fall Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Obtain a basic-to-intermediate understating of the logic of SEM and grasp the underpinning statistics of SEM.
2.  Critically read and evaluate empirical journal articles that use SEM.
3.  Develop the ability to apply SEM (and the correct model) to relevant questions in their area of research interest.
4.  Acquire Programming skills for conducting SEM.

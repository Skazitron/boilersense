HK 26900 - Career Development And Preparation For The Health And Fitness Fields

Credit Hours: 1.00.  Provides students with basic professional skills and materials for subsequent practicum experience and future employment search. Students will have secured a viable practicum site, complete with written job description/responsibilities, at the completion of the course. Typically offered Fall Spring.

    1.000 Credit hours


Syllabus Available

Levels: Graduate, Professional, Undergraduate 

Schedule Types: Distance Learning, Lecture 


Offered By: College of Health & Human Sci 

Department: Dept of Health & Kinesiology 


Course Attributes: Lower Division 


May be offered at any of the following campuses:      

      West Lafayette


Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Define and conceptualize individual areas of study as a professional discipline.
2.  Identify professional/certification organizations and journals relative to their area of study and the expectations of each throughout their career.
3.  Identify procedures for internship preparation.
4.  Write goals and objectives for personal and programming use (as to be applied in the internship).
5.  Develop a professional resume.
6.  Write a professional cover letter.
7.  Identify a career of interest and outline the knowledge and skill sets (experience) needed to get there.

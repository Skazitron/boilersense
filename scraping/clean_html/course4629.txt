PUBH 60400 - Public Health Administration

Credit Hours: 3.00.  (HK 68700) An introduction to the principals of management as applied to public health organizations, particularly local health departments. Topics include the organization of the U.S. public health system, legal and ethical obligations of public health administrators, the public health workforce and human resource issues, public health budgeting and finance, and leadership in the public health agency. The course is designed to introduce master's level students in public health to the management skills necessary to successfully implement a public health program. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Understand the transforming integrated health delivery system in the United States to build collaborative network of care providers.
2.  Formulate action plans for process improvement that addresses gaps of public health service areas.
3.  Appraise effectiveness of initiatives and programs to determine best practices and benchmarks for delivering public health services.

RUSS 38000 - Russian Culture And Civilization I

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course traces the intellectual and cultural development of Russia, starting with the founding of the Kievan state and continuing to the last days of the Russian Empire.  No knowledge of Russian is required.  Typically offered Fall, Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Understand the forces and circumstances (geographical, historical, political) that shaped the Russian state and gave rise to its distinctive forms of culture.
2.  Understand the distinction between rossiskoe (Russian as pertains to the polity) and russkoe (ethnically and linguistically Russian), and improve understanding of Russia as a multi-ethnic state. 
3.  Discover the contributions of non-ethnic Russians to Russian culture.
4.  Develop an appreciation for the artistic value of examples of Russian literature, music, and the pictorial arts.
5.  Increase ability to read critically and write persuasively.

BME 25600 - Physiological Modeling In Human Health

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Introduction to the physiology and medicine underlying practical problems in biomedical engineering, especially with respect to medical device development. Engineering skills taught and practiced within the context of human disease, injury, and illness on extended problem sets which include mathematical modeling and problem solving with appropriate documentation. Main physiological systems of focus are cardiovascular, pulmonary, and renal, and common afflictions thereof.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Understand the physiology and medicine underlying practical problems in biomedical engineering, especially with respect to medical device development.
2.  Understand the process of mathematical modeling of human anatomy and physiology, including technical description of objectives, methods, results, and conclusions from modeling exercises.

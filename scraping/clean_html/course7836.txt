HDFS 45800 - Development And Family Science Capstone Internship

Credit Hours: 6.00.  Supervised internship for the Developmental & Family Science major, doing work typical of an entry-level bachelor-degreed professional. Students will integrate and build upon knowledge developed during their studies in developmental and family science. Students will use and enhance their skills in child development, family and community health, or another area related to developmental and family science. Students will work in the placement setting secured during HDFS 45400. Must be a student in good academic standing during the internship semester. Permission of Department required. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Synthesize previously acquired and new knowledge about individual development, family processes, and broader social contexts and apply it to responsibilities in the internship setting.
2.  Demonstrate relevant workplace skills including direct service to clients, program development and evaluation, and/or other appropriate tasks.
3.  Exhibit professional work attitudes and behavior.

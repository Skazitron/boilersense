MGMT 42810 - Pricing Strategy And Analysis

Credit Hours: 3.00.  In the era of Big Data, where extensive information is available at consumer level, managers benefit from an increased ability to learn from their customers' behavior. In this scenario pricing becomes a crucial lever for increasing the relationship with the customer and maximizing profits and shareholder value. In this course, students will learn how to systematically approach pricing decisions, taking the perspective of the marketing manager. The course will use a combination of lectures, case discussions and in-class exercises to leverage micro-level transaction data for pricing decisions. Typically offered Fall Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Demonstrate the framework managers use to set the price of new and existing products.
2.  Demonstrate how to apply dynamic pricing and how to respond to psychological aspects related to pricing.
3.  Demonstrate when and how to apply customized pricing tactics such as price bundling and non-linear pricing.
4.  Demonstrate how to trigger and avoid price wars.

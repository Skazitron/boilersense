EAPS 60200 - New Graduate Student Seminar

Credit Hours: 1.00.  This course will provide students with the tools to succeed as a graduate student in the Department of Earth, Atmospheric, and Planetary Sciences, which include good communication with their advisors, graduate fellowship proposal writing, giving good talks and posters, review of regulations and graduate timelines, formulating a plan of study, and resources available to help gradate students succeed. Permission of Instructor.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Communicate effectively with their adviser.
2.  Organize their planned research into a graduate fellowship proposal.
3.  Provide and receive constructive criticism to and from colleagues.
4.  Understand how to give well organized talks and eye-catching posters for department seminars and national meetings.
5.  Assemble an advisory committee, formulate a plan of study, and know the department regulations and what is required to graduate in a timely manner.

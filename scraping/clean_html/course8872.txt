ECON 68500 - Experimental Economics I

Credit Hours: 1.00 to 3.00.  An overview of seminal and recent developments in experimental economic research. Provides practical training in designing and conducting experiments and the analysis of experimental data. Content varies from year to year. Applications include: industrial organization, game theory, finance, international trade, information economics, and monetary economics. Course readings are primarily from journal articles.  Prerequisite: ECON 60700. 61000.

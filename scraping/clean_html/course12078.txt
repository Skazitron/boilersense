EDCI 35004 - History, Culture, And Language In Tanzania

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course is a component of the Maymester in Tanzania study abroad program. In this course, students will study the history, culture, and language of Tanzania through experiences in local schools and the community, supplemented by course readings, assignments, discussions, and service learning projects. Typically offered Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Explore the history of Tanzania, including its identification as the cradle of mankind, colonization, independence, and the impacts of socialism and capitalism.
2.  Engage in cultural and linguistic immersion in a rural Tanzanian village and schools.
3.  Experience, first-hand, the educational system of Tanzania and read accounts of this system from a range of perspectives which draw attention to its strengths and challenges.
4.  Collaborate with Tanzanian residents on developing and implementing service learning projects in the community.

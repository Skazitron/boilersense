CGT 21600 - Vector Imaging For Computer Graphics

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Full-color vector illustrations for a variety of uses are produced using computer methods. Color theory, surface analysis, and rendering techniques are emphasized as they apply to vector-based illustrations.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Have completed all assignments associated with this course and earned a passing grade.
2.  Have reviewed all lectures online, attended all meeting sessions, and have participated in class discussions on a weekly basis.
3.  Be able to demonstrate their knowledge in all the areas listed within the course outcomes.
4.  Master the principles of creativity as they pertain to graphics design.
5.  Demonstrate their techniques for effective ideation and creative brainstorming.
6.  Execute design projects established in current multimedia technologies and best practices.
7.  Use appropriate design vocabulary utilizing the concepts of visual grammar and marketing language.
8.  Explain and demonstrate the importance of  deadlines in a multimedia production schedule. 
9.  Produce quality pieces for their student portfolios.

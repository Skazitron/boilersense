HK 30800 - Athletic Health Care

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Provides students with foundational knowledge and skills for the prevention, recognition, and management of common athletic injuries and illnesses, as well as emergency conditions encountered by sports medicine professionals. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

    3.000 Credit hours

May be offered at any of the following campuses:      

      West Lafayette


Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Identify basic components of an athletic injury/illness prevention program.
2.  Acquire knowledge related to athletic protective equipment and prophylactic braces.
3.  Develop an understanding of the physiological effects of injury in order to provide proper treatment and care.
4.  Develop practical knowledge of the mechanisms, signs, and associative anatomy for common athletic injuries associated with the upper extremity, lower extremity, head, spine, and thorax.
5.  Develop practical knowledge related to the recognition and treatment of environmental conditions. This will include an understanding of appropriate hydration requirements for physical activity.
6.  Develop an understanding of the acute care and emergency management of athletic injuries.



Prerequisites: 

 Undergraduate level  BIOL 20300 Minimum Grade of D- [may be taken concurrently]

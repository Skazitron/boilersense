NUTR 51300 - Food Chemistry

Credit Hours: 2.00.  Application of fundamental laws and concepts of chemistry, physics, and biology to foods and the sensory and analytical properties of food. Evaluation of the validity of health controversies related to food ingredients. Completion of a food additive assessment.

    2.000 Credit hours


Syllabus Available

Schedule Types: Distance Learning, Lecture

Department: Nutrition Science

      West Lafayette


Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Recognize and predict how chemistry structures and reactions influence sensory and analytical properties of food.
2.  Explain physical and chemical reactions in foods/beverages during preparation and processing techniques.
3.  Explain chemical function of food additives.
4.  Evaluate the validity of health controversies related to food ingredients.

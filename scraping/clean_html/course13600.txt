EDPS 61900 - Counseling Psychology Research Practicum

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Application of qualitative and quantitative research designs in counseling psychology. Concurrent prerequisite: EDPS 61800 and (PSY 60100 or STAT 50200) and Doctoral student standing enrolled in the Counseling Psychology Program. Typically offered Fall Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Gain knowledge of research methodologies in counseling psychology.
2.  Develop the ability to provide an evidentially based rationale for research, including consideration of multicultural issues.
3.  Display awareness of counseling psychology research.
4.  Demonstrate self-initiative and personal investment (a) in the research process and (b) in efforts to find and use relevant sources of information.
5.  Apply the scientific method in critiquing and generating research studies.
6.  Present research results in a manuscript that uses APA professional style.
7.  Enhance oral and written communication skills through presentation of material in a clear, organized, grammatically correct, and engaging manner.
8.  Develop professional writing and editing skills to prepare for careers as a counseling psychologist (e.g., scholarly contribution, reports).

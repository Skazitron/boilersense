VCS 23701 - Large Animal Nursing And Health Management For Veterinary Technicians II - DL

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This is the second of two courses involving nursing and health management of the large animal patient. This is the introduction to large animal patient care and treatment that a technician will be expected to provide in a veterinary practice. It includes techniques, equipment, client education, management of the hospitalized patient and preventative medicine. Emphasis will be on the role of the veterinary technician in the veterinary facility. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Explain fundamental individual patient management and nursing concepts to clients.
2.  Identify the concepts of nursing care and develop a care plan for the individual patient.
3.  Explain the basics of individual patient management of the large animal species.
4.  Present the fundamental techniques used in individual patient management.

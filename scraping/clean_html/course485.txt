ENGL 60811 - Entrepreneurship Communication

Credit Hours: 3.00.  The purpose of this class is to teach students how to use design thinking and information experience (IX) strategies to be innovative and generate new ideas for products and services. Students will learn how to write a business model and pitch that business model to investors or their supervisors. Permission of department required. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Identify and use written genres that are common in business start-ups.
2.  Use design thinking principles to envision new products and services, empathize with users, and ideate with new technology. 
3.  Analyze a market for your business and its products and services. 
4.  Develop a marketing strategy for raising awareness of your business. 
5.  Propose a new business concept by writing a business plan or model.

MET 53000 - Facilities Engineering Technology

Credit Hours: 3.00.  A study of the application of the engineering sciences and technology to the solution of problems associated with mechanical and electrical systems in buildings. Emphasizes commercial and industrial facilities. Identifying energy conservation measures for both mechanical and electrical systems and evaluating their economic impact are an important focus of the course. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Explain building energy trends and related policy.
2.  Explain the organizations and personnel supporting the built environment.
3.  Use building automation systems to monitor and control building operation.
4.  Quantify energy use in building systems.
5.  Use accepted professional practice for technical computations and writing.
6.  Calculate the economic impact of energy and resource conservation measures for buildings.
7.  Compute the impact of solar energy use for a building.
8.  Apply strategies for maintaining Indoor Air Quality in buildings.
9.  Evaluate the performance of mechanical or electrical systems in buildings.

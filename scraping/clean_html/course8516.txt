CGT 39500 - Smart Manufacturing Enterprise Organization And Operations

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course provides an introduction and overview of dealing with organizational change management needed to address existing cultures within organizations dealing with the digital transformation required to implement a Smart Manufacturing Enterprise. It introduces students to the cultural and organizational barriers that exist and the theories, tools, and best practices used to overcome them.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Summarize the barriers to organizational change management.
2.  Describe the tools, techniques and theories that are employed to overcome organizational and cultural barriers within and organization.
3.  Describe the impact that AI will have on the workforce and SMART manufacturing.
4.  Provide examples of the drivers, enablers and compelling forces for Industry 4.0's advancement.
5.  Define the Industry 4.0 framework and be able to outline the various systems used and their role in SMART Manufacturing organizations.
6.  Illustrate  the role and importance of IT+OT, Cyber Physical Systems, analytics, and BIG Data driving decisions in SMART manufacturing organizations.
7.  Identify the integration points of Machine-, Factory, and Enterprise-level activities using MES, FEMS, ERP, QMS, & PLM for connecting people, process, and machines across the manufacturing value chain.

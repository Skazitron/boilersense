MET 21300 - Dynamics

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Kinematics and kinetics principles of rigid-body dynamics are introduced. Emphasis is on the analysis of bodies in plane motion. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Identify the type of solution method required for dynamics problems.
2.  Use kinematics, work/energy principles, conservation of energy, impulse/momentum, or conservation of momentum to solve dynamics problems.
3.  Identify the correct coordinate system type for various dynamics problems.
4.  Calculate the mass moment of inertia for basic and composite shapes.
5.  Use mass moment of inertia when solving a dynamics problem.

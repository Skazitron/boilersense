SLHS 53300 - Medical Speech-Language Pathology

Credit Hours: 2.00.  Introduces the graduate speech-language pathology student to issues encountered in the medical environment in preparation for a healthcare externship and a career in the healthcare setting. Topics will include collaborative models in the medical setting, clinical documentation, ethical issues, equipment and instrumentation, medications, among others.  Typically offered Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Increase understanding of the medical speech-language pathology environment.
2.  Learn about various departments, professionals, and their collaborative roles with speech-language pathologists.
3.  Develop an understanding of solutions to ethical dilemmas related to the medical field.
4.  Understand and explain medications and their effects on swallowing and communication disordered individuals.
5.  Become familiar with equipment, instrumentation, and medical tests in the intensive care unit and other acute care settings.
6.  Discuss future trends related to the medical and speech pathology healthcare arena.
7.  Comprehend and explain information obtained from a medical record.

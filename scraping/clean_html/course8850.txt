ECON 63500 - Monetary International Economics

Credit Hours: 1.00 to 3.00.  A research seminar on international monetary issues. Topics include: theories of balance of payments and exchange-rate determination, international asset markets and capital flows, breakdowns of pegged exchange-rate regimes, currency substitution, monetary integration, international impacts of alternative monetary, and fiscal and intervention policies. Assignments are based on selected articles.  Prerequisite:  ECON 60800.

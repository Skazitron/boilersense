PUBH 60100 - Introduction To The Quantitative Methods Of Public Health

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Acquaints students with the basic concepts and methods of statistics, the applications, and their interpretation as used in public health. Students learn basic terminology and its meaning, how to calculate various statistical measures and indices, how to quantify health relationships, and how to compute and interpret inferential statistical techniques. Students will acquire the ability to utilize statistical software as a tool to facilitate the processing, editing, storing, displaying, analysis, and interpretation of health research-related data. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Acquaint students with the basic concepts and methods of statistics, their applications, and their interpretation as used in public health.
2.  Learn basic terminology and its meaning, how to calculate various statistical measures and indices, how to quantify health relationships and how to compute and interpret inferential statistical techniques.
3.  Acquire the ability to utilize the statistical software package SAS as a tool to facilitate the processing, editing, storing, displaying, analysis and interpretation of health research related data.

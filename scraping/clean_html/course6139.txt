VCS 14801 - Large Animal Nursing And Health Management For Veterinary Technicians I - DL

Credit Hours: 2.00.  This is the first of two courses involving nursing and health management of the large animal patient. This is the introduction to large animal patient care and treatment that a technician will be expected to provide in a veterinary practice. It includes techniques, equipment, client education, management of the hospitalized patient and preventative medicine. Emphasis will be on the role of the veterinary technician in the veterinary facility. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Discuss the impact of livestock health from "farm to fork", including markets.
2.  Explain fundamental livestock health management and nursing concepts to clients.
3.  Identify and discuss the commonalities and variations that exist between livestock species.
4.  Identify the concepts of nursing care and develop a care plan for the livestock patient.
5.  Know the basics of herd health management and regulatory issues of large animal species.
6.  Present the fundamental techniques used in herd health management.

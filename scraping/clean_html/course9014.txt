TECH 10500 - Introduction To Engineering Technology

Credit Hours:  3.00.  Introduction to the different disciplines incorporated in engineering technology as well as the skill set needed to be a successful student in engineering technology.  Focus will be on individual and professional development, problem identification, developing analytical skills, time and resource management, project planning, design, implementation and evaluation, and oral and written communication in the engineering technology profession. Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Understand systematic approach to problem solving and design.
2.  Understand measurement systems and the difference between fundamental and derived units.
3.  Convert units between measurement systems and within measurement systems.  
4.  Perform dimensional analysis on complex engineering equations.
5.  Understand significant figures, approximation, and rounding of results.
6.  Appreciate the use of triangle trigonometry in the analysis of engineering systems.
7.  Solve complex engineering equations.
8.  Interpolate tabulated data.
9.  Utilize spreadsheets for engineering calculations, data analysis, and data presentation.
10.  Understand how to set up a laboratory experiment, gather and analyze data, and present the results in a proper format.
11.  Students should understand professional registration and have an appreciation for professionalism and ethics in their careers.

ENTR 48100 - Consulting For Emerging Enterprises

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This advanced course requires students to apply the knowledge and skills learned in prerequisite entrepreneurship courses to a consulting project with a small business or non-profit. Student teams use a three-stage consulting model to guide contracting, data gathering, and creating value for the client. In-class time focuses on developing knowledge that they apply directly to the experiential consulting project. ENTR 48100 serves as a capstone course for the Certificate in Entrepreneurship and Innovation. Class size is limited. Permission of Instructor required. Typically offered Fall Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Strategically consider small business in a complex and changing environment.
2.  Apply a consulting model to establish priorities, set expectations, and develop recommendations as a member of a team.
3.  Foster relationships with business owners through building trust and sharing of information.
4.  Analyze operations to improve business viability and growth.
5.  Develop recommendations in the form of new methods, processes, systems, products and services.
6.  Generate deliverables for a client through a report and presentation.

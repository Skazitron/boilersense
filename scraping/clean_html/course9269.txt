ECET 27000 - Electronics Prototype Development And Construction

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course introduces project planning and basic concepts in electronic design automation (EDA). The student develops a portion of an electronic system by utilization of EDA, design for testing, design for manufacturability (DFM), and component selection techniques. New construction methods and testing techniques are introduced.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Design an electronic device or system to accomplish a given task.
2.  Demonstrate appropriate electronic assembly techniques.
3.  Develop and execute appropriate testing techniques on electronic devices and systems.
4.  Apply appropriate software applications to develop a prototype.
5.  Apply graphical communication techniques to convey technical information.
6.  Apply principles of design for manufacturability and design for testing to a project.
7.  Employ teaming methods on a project.

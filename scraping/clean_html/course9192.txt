GRAD 55000 - Fellowship And Grant Application Writing

Credit Hours: 2.00.  Fellowship Application Writing is open to any graduate student who is interested in applying for fellowships and other grant opportunities. Graduate students will be taught how to write personal statements, research statements, and extended research plans that they can submit for fellowship applications. Organization of research background, methods, and significance will be emphasized. Writing mechanics and addressing grant agency requirements and procedures will also be covered. Additionally, fellowship and grant opportunities will be discussed along with professional communication skills.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Analyze the different types of fellowship opportunities currently available to them as well as form broad knowledge of postdoc and faculty grants.
2.  Practice and evaluate proofreading skills to gain experience in editing fellowship application writings and be able to apply advanced proofreading skills to future writing projects.
3.  Compose persuasive documents using a grant proposal writing style with appropriate formatting and organization and analyze their writing with grant agency criteria.
4.  Synthesize and refine competitive personal statements, research plans, and statements of previous research to use for various fellowship applications.

NUR 41001 - Issues In Professional Nursing

Credit Hours: 2.00.  Promotes the use of critical thinking, clinical reasoning, and active learning to analyze contemporary issues related to professional nursing practice. Issues and key trends surrounding social determinants of health policy and economics, regulatory agencies, political action, cultural diversity, practice competency, nursing informatics and ethics are used as a framework. Permission of department required. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Analyze standards of nursing care and regulations related to credentialing and certification.
2.  Evaluate historical components and current nursing practice that impact healthcare systems.
3.  Demonstrate understanding of the impact of local, state, national and global regulations on healthcare trends and healthcare policy.
4.  Evaluate the impact that economic, political, cultural, and social concerns have on the role of professional nursing and the healthcare delivery system.
5.  Articulate the value of pursuing practice excellence, life-long learning, engagement, self care, and resilience in professional nursing practice.
6.  Utilize inquiry, analysis, information literacy, nursing informatics to address practice issues, ambiguity, and unpredictability in the healthcare systems.

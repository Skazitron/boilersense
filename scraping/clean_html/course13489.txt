EDPS 36300 - Psychoeducational, Medical, And Physical Aspects Of Individuals With Disabilities

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Introduction to characteristics of individuals with mild and severe intellectual disabilities: defining characteristics, etiological factors, assessment, social, legal and educational issues, and basic physical or medical disability management issues and techniques. Admission to Teacher Education Program, passage of Gates A required. Typically offered Fall.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Demonstrate the knowledge of the roles and responsibilities of the educational team.
2.  Demonstrate the knowledge of instructional strategies needed to promote independence.
3.  Demonstrate the knowledge of motor tone and motor patterns.
4.  Demonstrate the knowledge of proper positioning, lifting, and handling techniques.
5.  Demonstrate the knowledge of feeding and toileting techniques.
6.  Demonstrate the knowledge of infection control.
7.  Demonstrate the knowledge of assessment and monitoring techniques of major health conditions.

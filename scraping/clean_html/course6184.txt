VCS 80301 - Veterinary Clinical Skills I

Credit Hours: 2.00.   This course introduces students to the clinical environment in the Veterinary Teaching Hospital (VTH). Students will assist with treatments of patients and participate in activities of the various clinical services. Students will be assigned daytime and after-hours shifts. Laboratory sessions will address clinical skills, client communication skills, financial management, professionalism and wellness. Typically offered Fall.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Provide an opportunity for students to utilize their clinical skills in the VTH.
2.  Help students become familiar with the processes and protocols in the VTH.
3.  Allow students to experience the work done in the various services to help them in choosing clinical rotations for their 4th year.
4.  Administer treatments in the Veterinary Teaching Hospital under the supervision of technical staff.
5.  Enhance communication skills in teams.
6.  Perform dermatologic examinations in small animals and large animals.
7.  Practice a variety of clinical techniques in large and small animals such as jugular catheter placement, endotracheal intubation, abdominocentesis.
8.  Assemble an anesthetic machine and leak-test it.
9.  Manage client communications in an emergency situation.
10.  Explain how the economics of veterinary medicine impacts their future income.

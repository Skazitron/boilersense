PTEC 10800 - Guided Exploration

Credit Hours: 4.00 to 8.00.  The first in a series of transdisciplinary, real-world, project-based work and conversations exploring the intersections of humanities and technology, social and natural sciences. Projects emphasize design thinking; collaboration; communication; hands-on prototyping; testing; assessing. Learners will engage in individual and team project work and conversations that will change in scale and scope based on the learner's experience, goals, and learning needs. Permission of department required. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Demonstrate a developing level of understanding of issues related to the intersection of science, technology, and quantitative reasoning using project-based techniques to address unstructured, real-world problems.
2.  Demonstrate a developing level of understanding and skill with individual and team based project work.
3.  Demonstrate a developing level of understanding of issues related to the intersection of technology and culture using a variety of effective communication techniques.
4.  Demonstrate a developing level of information literacy using a variety of effective communication techniques.
5.  Demonstrate a developing level of understanding of connecting acquired knowledge and skills to the primary program competencies.

SOC 32700 - Crime, Deviance And Mass Media

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Various forms of mass media are used to explore the sociology of crime and deviance.  Topics may include white collar crime, juvenile delinquency, street crime, sexuality and sexual orientation, hate crimes, deviance and community.  Assignments include quizzes and short papers.  Typically offered Fall Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Able to identify concepts associated with specific theoretical perspectives and be able to describe these concepts.  
2.  Able to use these concepts to the media forms presented in the class.  
3.  Use the concepts to craft explanations for characters' actions in the fictional films. 
4.  Able to conduct analyses of the news that conform to accepted methodological standards of quantitative and qualitative research.

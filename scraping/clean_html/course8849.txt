ECON 63400 - International Trade

Credit Hours: 1.00 to 3.00.  Application of microeconomic techniques to international trade. Topics include: classical, neoclassical, and modern theories of international trade; the theory of tariffs and commercial policy, (including aspects of political economy), welfare aspects of trade and tariff theory, and related topics. The material is selected from current journal articles. Prerequisite:  ECON 60700.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Acquire the important building blocks for conducting both theoretical and empirical research in international trade. 
2.  Develop a keen understanding of the state of current research in trade and labor-market outcome. 
3.  Refine your skills in critical thinking and nourish your ability to initiate and execute independent inquiries.

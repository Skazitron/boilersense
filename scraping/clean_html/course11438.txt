PSY 12300 - Beyond Mental Health: The Science Of Well-Being

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course seeks to provide a scientific and psychological account of well-being - its definition, determinants, and outcomes. We address questions such as: How do we go about measuring happiness? Can happiness enhance longevity and health? What interventions can promote happiness? What types of societies are happier? This course will engage and challenge students not only intellectually, but also experientially as we work on enhancing happiness in our lives and in the lives of others around us. Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Outline the history of well-being research.
2.  Identify key antecedents of well-being.
3.  Explain and distinguish among different scientific theories of well-being.
4.  Describe the positive outcomes of happiness for individuals and societies.
5.  Discuss the scientific process of studying well-being.
6.  Apply positive psychology techniques to their own their lives.
7.  Identify and locate resources that promote mental health and well-being.

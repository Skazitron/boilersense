EDCI 64400 - Legal Issues In Career And Technical Education

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Comprehensive examination of contemporary federal, state, and local legislation; rules, policy, regulations, provisions, guidelines, and initiatives affecting the nature, establishment, and implementation of state and local programs at the secondary and postsecondary levels in career and technical education; need for legislation and initiatives and the reauthorization of existing legislation; social, political, and economic aspects of  legislation; advocacy for legislation; and public policy and policy development.  Approval of Department required.  Typically offered Fall Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Discuss the importance and impact of statutory law on the development and nature of career and technical education in the United States.
2.  Describe and contrast the legislative process and policies at the national and state levels.
3.  Review, articulate, and analyze critically the history of CTE and related legislation from 1862 to the present.
4.  Explain and apply the major provisions of the current CTE and workforce federal legislation.
5.  Relate and apply the content of the current Indiana State Plan for CTE to the provisions, requirements, and legal interpretation of Federal CTE legislation.
6.  Examine critically and apply Indiana legislation and initiatives that provide authority, support, structure, and administration for CTE in the State.
7.  Refine conceptual, analysis, synthesis, and evaluation skills.
8.  Enhance their written and oral communication skills.

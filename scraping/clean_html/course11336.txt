NUR 69020 - Leadership Seminar

Credit Hours: 1.00.  The nurse as leader is deconstructed and reconstructed within the realms of scientific knowledge, theory, education, and healthcare delivery. Nurse leaders as members of collaborative teams and the influence of nursing intelligence on research teams are examined. Historical and societal influences affecting nurse leaders are examined, including media portrayals of nursing, transformational leadership, critical theory and feminist theory. Models of leadership are explored within the context of scientific work; the student is challenged to evaluate themselves as present and future leaders and members of nursing's academe. Admission to the Nursing PhD program or instructor permission. This is one of the first courses taken by PhD students and may be open to graduate students outside the School of Nursing with permission from the instructor. Typically offered Fall Spring.

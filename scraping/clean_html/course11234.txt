NUR 51400 - Clinical Application In Pharmacotherapeutics For Pediatric Nurse Practitioners

Credit Hours: 1.00.  Applies information from NUR 50200, Pharmacotherapeutics in Primary Care, to the care of infants, children, and adolescents through the use of case studies.  Emphasis is placed on incorporating information from pharmacology, physiology, and physical assessment. Typically offered Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Discuss appropriate evidence based drug management regimens and alternatives for common disorders in the care of infants, children, adolescents, and young adults.
2.  Identify criteria for initiating and monitoring drug therapies with consideration to age, cost, gender, race, concurrent illness, potential adverse effects, and current research.
3.  Describe client education approaches to drug therapies commonly used in the care of infants, children, adolescents, and young adults.
4.  Incorporate legal and ethical concepts in planning pharmacologic management for infants, children, adolescents, and young adults.
5.  Identify appropriate resources in the delivery of pharmacologic regimens.

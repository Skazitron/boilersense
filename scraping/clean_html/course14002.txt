AD 31400 - Experimental Drawing

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Individual development of students' drawing abilities and skills at intermediate and advanced levels, emphasizing drawing as primary means of expression, experimentation with materials, and exposure to many different modes of creating in the area of drawing.

    3.000 Credit hours


Syllabus Available

Levels: Graduate, Professional, Undergraduate 

Schedule Types: Distance Learning, Studio 


Offered By: College of Liberal Arts 

Department: Patti & Rusty Rueff Schl DAP 


Course Attributes: Upper Division 


May be offered at any of the following campuses:      

      West Lafayette


Repeatable for Additional Credit: Yes - May be repeated an unlimited number of times


Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Communicate visually through the use of a variety of drawing materials, some structured assignments, and individual projects.
2.  Form an understanding of historical and contemporary artists' use of drawing as a primary means of expression.
3.  Build a vocabulary integral to the critical thinking process.

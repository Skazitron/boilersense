PHIL 56400 - Walk-Along Language Lab

Credit Hours: 1.00.  This course is a co-requisite, language-focused class linked to a philosophy seminar. Walk-along courses provide opportunities to shift focus from content to language form and use. Activities and projects for the course are designed to complement tasks and projects required for the seminar. Thus, the purpose of the walk-along course is to help you develop linguistic abilities required to succeed in your coursework. In addition, because of the centrality of writing for philosophy, walk-along courses will predominantly focus on developing your writing skills. Permission of department required. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Define core vocabulary of the seminar topic.
2.  Identify genre and rhetorical features of the seminar topic to aid reading comprehension and fluency.
3.  Compose several reaction papers for the seminar topic for a wide range of purposes, including explaining, criticizing, extending arguments, giving examples, etc.
4.  Explain and discuss your written works in class as well as in individual conferences.

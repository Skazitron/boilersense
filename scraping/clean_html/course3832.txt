AT 33800 - Airline Management

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course is a detailed study of airline management principles and processes. Topics include airline, economics, organization, forecasting, marketing, alliances, pricing, scheduling, finance, fleet planning, labor relations and air freight. Business ethics pertaining to airlines are introduced.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Explain the economic characteristics and the management structure of the commercial airline industry.
2.  Explain forecasting, marketing, pricing, scheduling, fleeting planning, labor relations, financing, and air cargo operations. 
3.  Demonstrate oral and written communication skills on topics related to the airline industry.
4.  Discuss global geography in relation to the international aviation industry.

EDCI 54500 - Planning In Career And Technical Education

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Principles and strategies in planning new and improving existing career and technical education programs, strategic planning, needs assessment, community and workforce market research, information and data analysis, program improvement.  Permission of Department required.  Typically offered Fall Spring.

    3.000 Credit hours


Syllabus Available

Schedule Types: Distance Learning, Lecture

Department: Curriculum and Instruction

May be offered at any of the following campuses:      

      West Lafayette


Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Develop an understanding of the principles and strategies in planning CTE and workforce education programs.
2.  Provide an overview of the CTE district program plan.
3.  Review Federal CTE and workforce education legislative planning regulations and guidelines.
4.  Examine State CTE and workforce education legislative planning regulations and guidelines.
5.  Critique various strategic planning models, strategies, and procedures.
6.  Describe strategies for advocating for diversity and access and equity for special needs populations in CTE program planning policy and practice.
7.  Evaluate local and CTE district planning factors and considerations.
8.  Discuss community needs assessment and workforce market research, information and data analysis, and use of findings in decision-making.
9.  Describe strategies for organizing and collaborating with program advisory committees.
10.  Explain continuous program evaluation and improvement.

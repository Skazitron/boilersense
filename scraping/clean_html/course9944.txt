POL 53100 - Terrorism And WMD Threat Assessment

Credit Hours: 3.00.  "Terrorism and WMD threat" assessment is designed to teach students how to analyze and assess the threats terrorist organizations present in the area of nuclear and radiological security and weapons of mass destruction more generally. The goal of this class is to enable students to identify, prioritize, and assess terrorist threats and propose appropriate, proportional, and cost-effective counter-measures. Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Understand the causes, motivations, strategies, and tactics of terrorist organizations, particularly regarding nuclear terrorism. 
2.  Understand challenges associated with implementing effective counterterrorism strategies and policies.  
3.  Analyze current and future nuclear proliferation threats from countries and non-state or sub-state actors and provide recommendations on how to address these security issues.  
4.  Define and analyze the various types of transnational threats and targets in order to craft effective policy responses.  
5.  Describe nuclear weapons proliferation, including incentives and disincentives for proliferation. 
6.  Analyze counter-proliferation strategies.

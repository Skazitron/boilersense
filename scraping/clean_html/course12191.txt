EDCI 55700 - Assessment Of Culturally And Linguistically Diverse Students

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Critical review of research and practice in language proficiency and content-area assessment of culturally and linguistically diverse students in K-12 education, and the sociocultural dimensions of standardized testing, academic achievement, and accountability. Critical analysis of standardized language proficiency and literacy tests. Development of authentic alternative language, literacy and content-area assessments. Prerequisites: Background in K-12 education. Typically offered Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Describe and critically analyze historical foundations, current research issues, national and state policies, and current practices in the testing and assessment of culturally and linguistically diverse students.
2.  Critically analyze the results of standardized language proficiency and academic achievement tests and their use in accountability programs.
3.  Design, utilize, and critically analyze the results of alternative authentic language and content-area assessments.
4.  Describe the impact of linguistic and cultural factors on the assessment process of linguistically diverse students.

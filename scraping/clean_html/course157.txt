ENGL 24900 - Great British Books

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Why are British books so compelling? This course examines the dynamic history of British literature and explores how canonical and contemporary texts shaped the cultural landscape of Britain, the British Empire, and the world. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Identify and make convincing arguments about major genres of British literature, including essays, journals, autobiographies, poetry, drama, and fiction.
2.  Identify the diversity of peoples, interests, and ideologies within British literature and culture, and the texts that define them.
3.  Define and analyze how literary texts both reflect and produce culture.
4.  Produce successful academic writing, including a debatable thesis, and appropriately presented evidence.

ECON 61200 - Macroeconomic Theory III

Credit Hours: 1.00 to 3.00.  Selected topics in contemporary research in macroeconomic theory. Emphasis on the implications of uncertainty in macroeconomic theory, including such topics as search theory, asset selection under uncertainty, stochastic optimal control, and asymmetric information.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Understand and define equilibrium in various models.
2.  Identify the sources of inefficiencies in a competitive equilibrium.
3.  Develop foundations in macroeconomic models necessary to pursue research in economics.

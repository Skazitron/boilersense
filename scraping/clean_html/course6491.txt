CSR 21500 - Textile Industry

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Fundamentals of the textile industry addressing the lifecycle of textile products beginning with the source and properties of textile fibers to the steps in the production of textile products and their impact on product performance and consumer satisfaction. Generate an awareness of the global textile industry as it relates to the impact on the environmental and social communities. Emphasis on retail, apparel, and interior design applications.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Predict fabric performance and end-use based on knowledge of fibers, yarns, fabric constructions, and finishes.
2.  Possess a working knowledge of the many facets of processing textiles from raw materials to the finished product, including laws and requirements that regulate textile labeling.
3.  Generate awareness of the scope and complexity of the global textile industry as well as the challenges it faces, environmentally and socially.
4.  Assess the impact of consumer buying behavior, use, care and disposal decisions on textiles and related industries.

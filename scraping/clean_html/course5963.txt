ENGR 29701 - Global Engineering Orientation

Credit Hours: 1.00.  Forum in preparation of study and work abroad. Understanding of cultural differences and collaborating across cultural boundaries, professionalism and ethics in foreign work and academic environments, leadership and team work options in foreign environments. Permission of instructor required. Typically offered Fall.

    1.000 Credit hours

      West Lafayette


Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Be provided with foundation in global engineering communication skills.
2.  Outline teamwork and leadership opportunities available through participation in the global engineering community.
3.  Be provided with globally diverse and multicultural experiences and discuss case studies to prepare them for upcoming international internships and semester study abroad.
4.  Identify resources available for academic studies and industrial work experience abroad.
5.  Develop a strong ethical framework and cultural sensitivity to prepare for the global professional workplace.

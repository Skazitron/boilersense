ANTH 31300 - Archaeology Of North America

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Archaeological overview of North America emphasizing Indigenous cultures prior to the arrival of Europeans, but including Contact and Post-Contact communities of the Historic Period.  Topics will include the peopling of the Americas, culture and environment, social complexity, and Cultural Resource Management.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Develop an understanding of the general historical outline for North America's past.
2.  Be able to summarize the diversity of peoples and cultures that existed in North America.
3.  Understand how North American archaeologists gather information and interpret the past.
4.  Be aware of how contemporary archaeological practice must take into account and communicate with other stakeholder's in north America's past.

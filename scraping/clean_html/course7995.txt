SLHS 54300 - Assessment And Treatment Of Literacy Disorders

Credit Hours: 2.00.  An introduction to the characteristics, definitions, etiologies, and assessment of literacy development in school-aged children (4-18 years of age).  Emphasis is placed on the scope of practice for speech-language pathologists in the assessment and intervention process for children diagnosed with language based literacy disorders. Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Differentiate between normal and disordered literacy development.
2.  Identify formal and informal assessment tools in each of the following areas: phonological processing, decoding, encoding, oral reading fluency, reading comprehension.
3.  Summarize literacy strengths and weaknesses in clients with various profiles.
4.  Identify appropriate strategies / techniques to remediate client weaknesses.
5.  Identify various organizations, websites, programs, and supporting research for parents and educators.

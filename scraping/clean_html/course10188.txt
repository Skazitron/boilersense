PHIL 55300 - Mathematical Logic

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This class is an introduction to various logical foundations of mathematics as well as the basics of logical metatheory. Typically offered Fall Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Understand some of the more advanced methods employed in logical analysis.
2.  Explain important problems and issues in foundational mathematical logic and express them in clear and precise terms.
3.  Analyze and assess foundational results in mathematical logic.
4.  Explain, apply, and appraise the use of formal languages.
5.  Assess and construct precise, abstract arguments using the tools of mathematical logic.  (This will help students engage in better written and oral communication, in particular in circumstances where perspicuity and logicality are of special importance.)

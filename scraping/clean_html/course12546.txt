FVS 33700 - Editing I

Credit Hours: 3.00.  The course introduces students to the principles of film and video editing. Projects, discussions, demonstrations, and hands-on exercises expose students to the technical and artistic aspects of the editing process. Film & Video Studies and Film & Theatre Productions majors have priority. Typically offered Fall Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Establish an understanding of the history of editing and post production as it relates to Film and Video technology. 
2.  Examine the basic concepts involved in the editing process. 
3.  Understand and demonstrate basic editing techniques. 
4.  Develop proficiency with industry standard software. 
5.  Identify methods to increase speed and effectiveness of non-linear editing.
6.  Execute an example of the editing process under strict time constraints.

CNIT 53000 - Information Technology Business Analysis

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course introduces the application of knowledge, skills, tools, and techniques that business analysts use to solve business problems related to information technology projects. Special emphasis is placed on learning and applying the concepts of enterprise analysis, business analysis planning, stakeholder management, requirements management, and solution evaluation. Permission of department required.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Discuss the pros and cons in intelligent discourse, of the need for IT business analyst skillsets in information technology projects.
2.  Research and assess the various means to construct a business case and business analysis plan to justify the execution of a project and then plan the business analyst's activities on the project.
3.  Research and assess the various elicitation, analysis, specification and validation approaches to requirements development in real life business problems.
4.  Select, defend and utilize business analysis tools and techniques in the context of information technology projects.
5.  Research and contribute to the business analysis body of knowledge as it can be applied to information technology projects.

HK 33100 - Developmentally Appropriate Physical Activity For Children

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Introduction to the developmental stages of locomotor, non-locomotor, and manipulative skills in children. Includes the assessment of children's motor skills and physical fitness levels, and designing developmentally appropriate physical activities for children. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Describe research supporting the need for physical activity in children.
2.  Describe and detect the stages of locomotor, non-locomotor, and manipulative motor skill development in children.
3.  Design developmentally and age appropriate activities to enhance children's locomotor, non-locomotor, and manipulative motor skills.
4.  Design skill progressions and instructional cues to help children develop effective motor skills.
5.  Identify guidelines for developmentally appropriate physical activity/exercise in children. 
6.  Design and implement developmentally appropriate rhythmical activities for children.
7.  Identify and develop effective means of assessing children's motor skill development and physical fitness levels.

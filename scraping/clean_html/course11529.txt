PSY 43400 - Neurobiology Of Disease

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course explores the neurobiological basis of disorders of the nervous system and psychiatric disease. Students will learn various neurological disorders that affect behavior, the associated cellular and molecular mechanisms, and current treatment strategies. Typically offered Fall.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Demonstrate how to search for scientific literature and compare/contrast peer-reviewed articles with news media.
2.  Identify, discuss, examine, and critically evaluate the basic mechanisms and clinical features of diseases of the nervous system.
3.  Describe research on the biology of various neurological and psychiatric disorders.
4.  Discuss and critique research papers on ground breaking discoveries related to underlying mechanisms and treatments of various neurological and psychiatric disorders.
5.  Generate analogies and craft models that represent human neurological and psychiatric diseases.

AD 69600 - Aesthetic Development In Children

Credit Hours: 3.00.  (EDCI 63100) Examination of historical and contemporary theory and research in children's aesthetic development (response and production) and analysis of the influences of such development on art education curricula and methodology.  Permission of Instructor required.  Prerequisite:  BA in Art Education or an equivalent area.

    3.000 Credit hours


Syllabus Available

Schedule Types: Lecture

May be offered at any of the following campuses:      

      West Lafayette


Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Examine historical and contemporary conceptions of the development of children's responses to works of art.
2.  Examine historical and contemporary conceptions of the development of children's art production.
3.  Analyze the influence of children's aesthetic development (in response and production) upon art education curricula and methodology.

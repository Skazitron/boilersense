MGMT 52610 - Data-Driven Marketing

Credit Hours: 2.00. The primary focus of this course will be on what insights marketers typically seek to gather from customer, company, and competitive data and how to integrate the insights gathered from data for marketing decisions.  The course will also provide students with a review of the best practices across a variety of industries and companies through case studies.  Finally, the course will include guest presentations by senior executives from the three major data-driven marketing firms who complete in the USA and worldwide. Typically offered Fall Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Apply concepts learned in Marketing Analytics and other classes to real world problems to gain experience in data collection and preparation, descriptive analytics and predictive analytics. Use SAS Enterprise Miner software.
2.  Gain practical knowledge in essential marketing metrics and measurement, customer value and CRM, segmentation, market basket analysis, text mining, web analytics, and marketing automation.
3.  Learn how a combination of technology, data mining and analytical techniques can be used to gain customer insights, and make marketing decisions that are backed by hard evidence.
4.  Be exposed to the challenges of working in teams to achieve marketing campaign goals in real world situations.

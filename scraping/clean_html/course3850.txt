AT 38201 - Advanced Flight Instructor Lecture

Credit Hours: 3.00.  A discussion of instructional techniques for flight and ground instruction with a focus on instrument and multi-engine flight. This course includes analysis of maneuvers, aircraft performance, and federal aviation regulations applicable to flight instructors. Some practice teaching will be required. Preparation for the FAA practical tests for instrument instructor and multi-engine instructor are included.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Understand and explain the definitions, objectives, elements, and common errors for all items listed in the FAA practical test standards.
2.  Understand and demonstrate the learning process, elements of effective teaching, student evaluation and testing, course development, lesson planning, and classroom training techniques.
3.  Understand the privileges and limitations, record keeping, and eligibility requirements applicable to certified flight instructors.
4.  Appropriately utilize flight instructor endorsements for private pilots, instrument rated pilots, commercial pilots, and multi-engine rated pilot applicants.

EDCI 67400 - Advanced Instructional Design Theory

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Examines the impact of both psychological learning theories and instructional applications upon the development of various instructional design theories. Emphasis is on theory construction, influence of learning and educational principles, and the current and future possibilities of instructional design theory.  Prerequisite:  EDCI  53100 and 57200. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Identify the key principles and underlying assumptions from several prominent Instructional Design Theories.
2.  Compare and contrast various ID theories and explain their individual strengths and weaknesses.
3.  Determine when, why, how, and under what conditions specific ID theoretical principles can be applied to resolve applied learning problems.
4.  Assess the effectiveness of specific ID theories and propose alternative viewpoints, guidelines, and perspectives to enhance learning given specific situations.

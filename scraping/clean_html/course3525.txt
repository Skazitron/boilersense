COM 44400 - Introduction To Communication And Social Entrepreneurship

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This class introduces students to the roles that communication and innovation play in social entrepreneurship. It explores activities of entrepreneurs, ethics and skill development at the individual and organizational levels, and communication functions in not-for-profit and social venture space.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Identify the major components of communication and social change.
2.  Describe the social economy.
3.  Articulate how both social entrepreneurship and social innovation benefit society.
4.  Describe the difference between nonprofit organizations (NPs), social action efforts, NGOs and social enterprises, as well as traditional entrepreneurship, social entrepreneurship, social intrapreneurship and social innovation.
5.  Explain how social enterprises function in different sectors and regions, of various sizes, with vastly different missions.
6.  Articulate the challenges social entrepreneurs face as they startup, develop, and communicate with various publics.
7.  Identify the best approaches and communication tools that can be deployed to assist in "getting out the message" about NP goals and objectives.

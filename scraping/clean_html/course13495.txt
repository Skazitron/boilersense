EDPS 40300 - Individual Social, Emotional, Behavioral Supports

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course is designed to prepare students with advanced knowledge and skills in designing and delivering individualized social-emotional and behavioral supports for learners. Emphasis will be placed on functional behavior assessment and function-based interventions and supports. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Identify the advanced principles of behavior and provide classroom examples.
2.  Articulate the connection between social-emotional skills and challenging behavior.
3.  Define learner behaviors.
4.  Select an appropriate behavior measurement system for learner outcomes.
5.  Measure and graph learner behavior.
6.  Conduct a functional behavior assessment.
7.  Develop and implement a behavior support plan.
8.  Analyze learner data to guide educational programming decisions.

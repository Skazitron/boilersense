AT 14502 - Private Pilot Flight Under Federal Aviation Regulations Part 141

Credit Hours: 2.00.  In this course, the student will receive the necessary dual flight instruction and solo flight time to qualify for the FAA Private Pilot Certificate under Part 141 of the Federal Aviation Regulations. Permission of department required.

    2.000 Credit hours

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Demonstrate proficiency in takeoffs, landings.
2.  Demonstrate proficiency in flight at minimum controllable airspeed including stalls and recoveries. 
3.  Demonstrate proficiency in planning and execution of cross-country flights using various means of navigation.
4.  Demonstrate proficiency in night operations and simulated instrument flight. 
5.  Demonstrate proficiency in abnormal and emergency procedures.



Prerequisites: 

 Undergraduate level  AT 10100 Minimum Grade of D- [may be taken concurrently] or Undergraduate level  AT 14400 Minimum Grade of D- [may be taken concurrently]

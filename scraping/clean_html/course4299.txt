MUS 38300 - Digital Audio Recording And Production I

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course is an introduction to digital audio recording and production practices. Topics covered will include design and structure of sound studios, musical sequencing and recording, microphones, fundamentals of sound reinforcement, and sound editing. Students will be required to develop music recording projects.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Explain the fundamentals of musical acoustics.
2.  Define digital audio and MIDI (Musical Instrument Digital Interface) terminology.
3.  Explain and demonstrate the principles of music recording (studio and live mixes).
4.  Explain and demonstrate the fundamentals of sound reinforcement (live sound performances).
5.  Explain and demonstrate microphone selection and placement.
6.  Design a recording studio.
7.  Explain and demonstrate musical sequencing and digital audio editing.
8.  Explain and demonstrate how to combine digital sequenced data with natural acoustic sounds.
9.  Create professional mixes: adding reverb, compression, special effects, etc.

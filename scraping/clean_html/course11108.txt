NUR 22601 - Topics In Nursing Research And Evidence-Based Practice

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Introduces foundational competencies for evidence-based practice (EBP), including computer literacy, information literacy, and professional communication. The principles of research process and strengths and limitations of research articles in relation to EBP in nursing and healthcare are identified. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Describe the principles of research and the process of EBP in relation to nursing and healthcare.
2.  Use information and information technologies ethically, legally, and proficiently according to Information Literacy Competency Standards for Higher Education.
3.  Explain the purpose and methodology of various types of quantitative and qualitative research designs in nursing and healthcare.
4.  Evaluate the quality of research evidence to determine scientific merit, strengths and limitations relevant to clinical research.
5.  Examine economic, legal, and ethical issues related to conduction research.
6.  Discuss the process of translating research evidence into practice.
7.  Demonstrate the characteristics of an innovator that are necessary for EBP, which include leadership, a sense of inquiry, flexibility to change, awareness of self and of the environment, effective communication, critical thinking, lifelong learning, and professionalism.

AD 36101 - The Constructed Image

Credit Hours: 3.00.  An intermediate level course focusing on the production of creative images constructed in the studio and in the field. Projects will address the planning and design of photographic images through the use of set design, styling, and post-production. Students will investigate both historical and contemporary trends in the art of the photographically-constructed image.

Learning Outcomes: 
1. Construct and compose images using set design, styling, collage, and photomontage.
2. Develop communication skills to successfully interpret proposals and instructions from clients.
3. Analyze theoretical and historical concerns regarding constructed images and demonstrate an understanding of those concerns.
4. Develop visual communication and conceptual image making skills.
5. Develop techniques of creating photographically constructed images and demonstrate an understanding of those techniques.

NUR 22501 - Nursing Practice Foundations II: Fundamentals

Credit Hours: 4.00.  Designed for second degree students, this course continues to expand on the professional nurse role supported by fundamental concepts and principles of the nursing process in lecture and clinical settings. Learners apply communication, assessment and nursing skills while caring for the needs of clients in diverse populations across the lifespan. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Apply the nursing process to clients representing healthy to chronically ill populations.
2.  Use beginning critical thinking and clinical judgment when providing safe, quality nursing care to patients in the clinical setting.
3.  Apply culturally sensitive clinical interventions using evidence-based-practice (EBP).
4.  Apply principles of physical assessment and history taking to develop plans of care using the nursing process for clients across the lifespan.
5.  Evaluate the effectiveness of nursing interventions on short-term and long-term client goals.
6.  Develop appropriate evaluation criteria to determine clinical outcomes that meet professional standards of care.
7.  Identify client adaptation and coping strategies when adjusting to changes along the health-wellness continuum.

AMST 21000 - Sport In American Culture

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course engages critical questions regarding sport and its role in American culture. It examines the ways sport serves as a key site for the study of larger social issues and social problems, including how race, gender, social class and other social locations are simultaneously reproduced and challenged in and through sport. The course also explores the relationships between sport and other major social institutions, such as the economy, education, politics, and media.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Understand sport as a social construction. Understand sport as a contested activity.
2.  Identify and explain the relationships of sport to other institutions in society, including politics, education, economy, and the media.
3.  Explain the role of sport in central social issues, including race/racism, gender/sexism, class, homophobia, and violence.
4.  Apply academic theories and concepts to the study of sport.

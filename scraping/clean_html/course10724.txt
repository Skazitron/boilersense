ENTR 48200 - Venture Planning Capstone

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This advanced course requires students to apply the knowledge and skills learned in prerequisite entrepreneurship courses to the business planning process. Students work on multidisciplinary teams to develop a new venture of their own. Throughout the semester, they present their ideas to both peers and experts from the community who provide feedback and insight into what it takes to launch a successful business. ENTR 48200 serves as a capstone course for the Certificate in Entrepreneurship and Innovation. Typically offered Fall Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Refine an innovative and viable business concept and model.
2.  Validate an addressable market through research and competitive analysis.
3.  Solicit and integrate feedback from practicing entrepreneurs, investors and advisors.
4.  Select sources of financing based on their influence on business startup and growth.
5.  Apply methods to attract, engage, and secure customers.
6.  Create, present and critique business plans and pitches.

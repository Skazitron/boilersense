ECON 69100 - Advanced Topics In Panel Data

Credit Hours: 2.00 or 3.00.  This course covers selected topics in the econometrics of panel data with an emphasis on developing a clear understanding of the methodological issues involved as well as identifying a set of substantive empirical issues where such methods can be applied. Pre-requisite: ECON 67100 and ECON 67200.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Organize data into an easy to interrupt manner. 
2.  Understand the role of factor models as as a tool for dimension reduction in the analysis of large-dimensional data as well as a device for modeling cross-sectional correlation in panel data analysis when the cross-sectional and time series dimensions are comparable in magnitude.

MGMT 45600 - Legal Foundations For Business II

Credit Hours: 3.00.  A study of commercial law as prescribed by the Uniform Commercial Code, including the law of sales, documents of title, negotiable instruments, and the law relating to security interests. Additional material is presented covering the legal aspects of real estate transactions and personal property transfer. Typically offered Fall Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Have a good basic understanding of the structure of the U.S. legal system.
2.  Be able to understand and apply basic legal terms and concepts in almost every area of the law.
3.  Be able to recognize common legal and ethical issues which will arise in the course of doing business.
4.  Demonstrate your knowledge in classroom discussion and through the four (4) exams given during the semester.
5.  Have the ability to more effectively communicate with and assist counsel on legal matters in your future business career.

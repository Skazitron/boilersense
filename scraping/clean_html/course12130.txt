EDCI 45000 - Techniques Of Coordination In Cooperative Vocational Education

Credit Hours: 2.00.  For prospective and present coordinators and supervisors of cooperative vocational programs in public schools. Emphasis on problems of organization, administration, supervision, and operation of cooperative education programs. Typically offered Summer Fall Spring.

    2.000 Credit hours


Syllabus Available

Levels: Graduate, Professional, Undergraduate 

Schedule Types: Distance Learning, Lecture 


Offered By: College of Education 

Department: Curriculum and Instruction 


Course Attributes: Upper Division 


May be offered at any of the following campuses:      

      West Lafayette


Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Understand the role of Work Based Learning in the secondary school system.
2.  Understand the relationships between co-op programs and other career and technical education programs and academic programs.
3.  Identify and investigate current national and state trends in Work Based learning.
4.  Develop marketing and promotional materials about co-op programs.
5.  Understand the required forms necessary for co-op programs.
6.  Understand the role of the teacher coordinator.
7.  Understand the role of the employer.
8.  Recognize methods of evaluation of co-op programs.
9.  Understand the importance of the calendar of events for operating a co-op program.
10.  Understand teacher coordinator activities before instruction begins.
11.  Understand the importance of the related class to the co-op.
12.  Understand the legal issues involved in co-op programs.

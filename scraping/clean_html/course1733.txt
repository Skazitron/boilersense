ILS 63000 - Digital Humanities Foundations

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course introduces graduate students to the core theoretical and practical components of Digital Humanities. Students engage key theoretical texts in weekly seminars and practice central methodologies in weekly work sessions. Typically offered Fall Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Learn the basic vocabulary and concepts in digital humanities.
2.  Gain familiarity with digital humanities projects, critical work, resources in the field, and hands-on experience of building a scholarly digital research project.
3.  Gain experience working collaboratively with others on a joint project.
4.  Perform critical, formal analyses of DH projects, digital media, and digital tools.
5.  Write focused, analytical essays in clear, grammatical prose.
6.  Employ logic, creativity, and interpretive skills to produce original, persuasive arguments.
7.  Gain familiarity with digital tools and media used in in the field of digital humanities to create new knowledge of humanities subjects.

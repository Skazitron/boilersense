MFET 15900 - Introduction To The Smart Manufacturing Enterprise

Credit Hours: 1.00.  This course serves as an introduction to modern smart manufacturing methods and processes.  The course explores the manufacturing enterprise in different industry contexts to illustrate multiple, varied paths to inform students of multiple career opportunities. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Define the multiple areas that rely on the manufacturing process and connected enterprise. (Case studies, guest speakers)
2.  Demonstrate understanding of the fundamentals of historical technology evolution and potential future trends of manufacturing through Industry 1.0, Industry 2.0, Industry 3.0, Industry 4.0, etc.
3.  Identify potential manufacturing methods and processes in modern smart manufacturing. (Case studies, guest speakers)
4.  Apply knowledge of different manufacturing methods to design a prototype of a manufacturing process. (prototyping and presentation)

MFET 34100 - Process And Continuous Control Applications

Credit Hours: 3.00.  An examination of devices, algorithms, strategies, and standards used in smart manufacturing continuous process and control serves as the basis of this course. Concepts include Proportional, Integral, & Derivative Control (P&ID), loop modeling, transfer functions, PID design, implementation and tuning. Course laboratories focus on implementing and tuning systems using industry-grade hardware and devices. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Explain the importance of control systems in the management of a continuous process.
2.  Apply fundamental control theory concepts and models to a process or continuous control scenario.
3.  Document process control applications, using recognized standards and symbology.
4.  Apply controller algorithms and tuning methods to refine the performance of control loops.
5.  Determine appropriate control architecture and hardware for single and multiple loop systems.

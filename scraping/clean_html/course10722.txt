ENTR 48000 - Entrepreneurship Capstone

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This advanced course focuses on entrepreneurial career development. It requires students to apply the knowledge and skills learned in prerequisite entrepreneurship courses to the development of their professional goals and networks. They explore how they can use their entrepreneurial talents, skills and interests in a variety of ways, including launching a start-up, joining a family business, or working for an established organization. ENTR 48000 serves as a capstone course for the Certificate in Entrepreneurship and Innovation. Typically offered Fall Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Examine one's entrepreneurial strengths and interests that shape career decisions.
2.  Create a personal business plan to guide career development.
3.  Differentiate how entrepreneurship knowledge and skills are applied in various contexts, including family business and social ventures.
4.  Demonstrate ways to apply entrepreneurial skills within established organizations.
5.  Practice negotiation and selling skills.
6.  Build a personal brand through professional networks and social media.

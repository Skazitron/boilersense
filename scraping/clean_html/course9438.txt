SFT 23000 - Evolution Of Space Power

Credit Hours: 1.00.  An exploration of Space Force heritage and leaders and an examination of distinctive space power capabilities and functions. Its purpose is to instill an appreciation of space power development and employment, while continuing to develop cadet communication skills.

    1.000 Credit hours


Syllabus Available

Levels: Graduate, Professional, Undergraduate 

Schedule Types: Distance Learning, Lecture

Department: Division of Military Science 


Course Attributes: Lower Division 


May be offered at any of the following campuses:      

      West Lafayette


Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Communicate the evolution of rocketry and space power throughout history.
2.  Describe the employment of space power from an institutional, doctrinal, and historical perspective.
3.  Explain the similarities and differences between the US space program and international partners and competitors.
4.  Apply the Full-Range Leadership Model to Space force scenarios.

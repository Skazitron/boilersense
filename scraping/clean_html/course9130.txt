VM 22900 - Laboratory Animal Health For Veterinary Technicians - DL

Credit Hours: 2.00.  This course will provide instruction in the concepts of Laboratory Animal Health Management to the veterinary technician student. This course will place an emphasis on the information needed to work with laboratory animals as well as issues that apply to laboratory animal medicine along with the care of laboratory animals and exotics and specific procedures that are used in laboratory animal and exotic pet medicine. Students are expected to achieve a level of understanding of laboratory animal care and management so that they will be able to communicate effectively with the veterinarian and other individuals. Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Explain the concepts of animal welfare and animal rights.
2.  Introduce the student to the basic concepts of laboratory animal medicine.
3.  Explain basic laboratory animal housing and environmental control concepts.
4.  Identify the basic concepts of experimental methodology.
5.  Introduce the student to the different laboratory animals and exotic pocket pets.
6.  Explain basic restraint, husbandry, and anesthesia of laboratory animals and pocket pets.

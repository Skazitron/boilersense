LATN 31500 - Latin Paleography I

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course gives students the opportunity to work with original medieval Latin manuscripts, involving transcription and translation, as well as study of the creation, care, and preservation of rare manuscripts. Typically offered Fall Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Work with unedited and as yet untranslated Latin texts.
2.  Transcribe Latin documents and produce original translations.
3.  Address and solve complex syntactical challenges.
4.  Identify fields tangential to Latin and Classics discipline, such as mediaeval history, art history, religious and biblical studies, music, etc.
5.  Demonstrate an understanding of digital humanities.



Prerequisites: 

 Undergraduate level  LATN 20200 Minimum Grade of D-

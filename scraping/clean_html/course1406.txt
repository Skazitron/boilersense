MGMT 22000 - Making The Business Case

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course develops one's ability to address situations faced by managers. This involves collecting information, analyzing that information to determine what decision or action is required and then selling the recommended decision or action to the management team. Selling this is done using one's persuasive skills in both telling a story and illustrating that story. Typically offered Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Differentiate whether a situation calls for making a decision or solve a problem.
2.  Identify important facts in case studies.
3.  Organize facts found in case studies into issues that factor in the analysis of alternatives.
4.  Evaluate alternative solutions to a decision in terms of how they address issues.
5.  Recommend an alternative solution.
6.  Create a plan of action that will put the recommendation in place.
7.  Create presentations that have a story line flowing through them.
8.  Create a slide deck that supports the story line of the presentation.
9.  Demonstrate effective speaking and body language when making presentations.

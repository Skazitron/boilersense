PUBH 36500 - Public Health Research Methods

Credit Hours: 3.00.  The course is designed to help you (1) develop an understanding of the nature of research and the scientific method in public health, (2) acquire an appreciation for the different types of research conducted within public health, and (3) provide an overview of the ethical, conceptual, and logistical considerations involved in designing, conducting, and critically evaluating public health research in basic and applied settings.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Explain and discuss the public health research process.
2.  Identify or develop sound public health research questions and hypotheses, and discuss appropriate methods for testing the hypotheses.
3.  Demonstrate appropriate techniques regarding data collection in public health.
4.  Interpret research findings and discuss their implications relative to public health.

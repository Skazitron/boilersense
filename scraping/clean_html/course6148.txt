VCS 23001 - Small Animal Nursing For Veterinary Technician II - DL

Credit Hours: 2.50.  This is the second of two courses involving nursing and management of the small animal patient. This course continues the concepts of small animal patient care and treatment that a technician will be expected to provide in a veterinary practice. It includes techniques, equipment, client education, management of the hospitalized patient and preventative medicine. Emphasis will be on the role of the veterinary technician in the veterinary hospital. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Demonstrate the basics of small animal nursing care.
2.  Present the fundamental techniques used in small animal nursing care.
3.  Introduce the nursing aspects of managing common small animal diseases.
4.  Introduce small animal breeds.
5.  Present the fundamental techniques involved with critical care and management of common diseases of small animals.

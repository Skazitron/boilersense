MSL 35000 - American Military History And Leadership

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course provides a historical perspective for the decisions made by American military leaders. In addition, the course examines the external political and social environments within which the military, and in particular its leaders, operate. Emphasis is placed on using common themes that relate to the American experience in war to determine how periods of peace and war challenged the nation and influenced the development of its military policy. This course is required for Army ROTC cadets but it open for all students on campus. Typically offered Fall Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Examine the evolution of war and the progression of military professionalism.
2.  Evaluate the common themes in American military history.
3.  Discuss the heritage that comes with pursuing a career in the profession of arms.
4.  Describe how political influences affect the evolution of military policy.
5.  Explain the relationship between military and civilian hierarchies of command.
6.  Analyze the role of leadership in preparing the military for its next war.
7.  Evaluate the relationship between war and society through the lens of the U.S. military.
8.  Compare/contrast lessons learned from military history to the contemporary operating environment.

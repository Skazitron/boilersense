ECE 41200 - Introduction To Engineering Optics

Credit Hours: 3.00.  The control and characteristics of optical radiation are covered. Applications to optical instrumentation, thin films, holography, and polarizing optics are discussed.

    3.000 Credit hours

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Analyze simple optical systems (dielectric slabs, thin lenses, reflectors).
2.  Model Gaussian beams and the transformation of beams.
3.  Analyze and design polarization systems, including polarizers and wave plates.
4.  Know optical interferometers and the operations of interferometers such as the Michelson, Fabry-Perot and double-slit.
5.  Analyze diffraction patterns.

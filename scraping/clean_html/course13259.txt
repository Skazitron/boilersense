NUCL 65500 - Two-Phase Flow Computational Fluid Dynamics Applications

Credit Hours: 3.00.  The course guides the student to two-fluid model solutions using computational fluid dynamics (CFD). Topics include: review of the two-fluid model, review of elliptic and parabolic partial differential equations, description of numerical algorithms, and two-phase flow channel and jet flows.  Prerequisite: NUCL 55100 or ME 50600. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Understand basic principles of hyperbolic and parabolic partial differential equations applied to the equations of fluid dynamics.
2.  Use finite differences to solve partial differential equations of fluid dynamics.
3.  Apply principles in 1 and 2 to simulations with Navier Stokes and Two Fluid Model Equations.
4.  Employ stability theory to analyze the Two Fluid Model.

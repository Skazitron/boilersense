ECON 67100 - Econometrics I

Credit Hours: 1.00 to 3.00.  An introduction to the modern techniques of econometrics and their applications. Topics include: the classical linear regression model (specification, estimation, inference, and prediction), specification analysis, functional form, heteroscedasticity, and autocorrelation.   Prerequisite: ECON 67000.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Investigate the properties of econometric estimators under a variety of conditions.  
2.  Explain econometric techniques for estimating causal effects. 
3.  Explain the principles and purpose of Monte Carlo simulation methods. 
4.  Conduct basic empirical analyses of cross sectional data.

BCHM 61100 - Chromatin Biology And Chromosome Dynamics

Credit Hours: 2.00.  An exploration of current models and recent discoveries in chromatin biology and the relationship between chromatin and gene expression,  as well as other aspects of chromosome structure and function. Prerequisites: BCHM 56100 and BCHM 56200 or BCHM 60501.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Basic knowledge of the molecular mechanisms in gene expression and regulation.
2.  Basic knowledge of the molecular mechanisms of chromatin modification and epigenetic processes.
3.  Appreciation for post-transcriptional gene regulatory events.
4.  Enhancement of oral and written communication skills.
5.  Mastery of reading and interpreting scientific literature in gene expression fields.
6.  Development of critical thinking and creativity in scientific research.

EDCI 68200 - Contemporary Curriculum Theory

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Critical examination of contemporary concepts of curriculum theory. Rational/technical curriculum, social needs/child centered curriculum. Socio-political curriculum theory; curriculum as existential, aesthetic, and phenomenological. Implications for current practice, school reform, and educational research.  Typically offered Spring.

    3.000 Credit hours

May be offered at any of the following campuses:      

      West Lafayette


Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Develop an understanding of the social theories and philosophies that inform contemporary curriculum theorizing.
2.  Articulate the role of curriculum theorizing in defining and analyzing educational phenomena.
3.  To utilize curriculum theory toward the development of educative responses to issues of ethics, equity, power, and privilege.
4.  To synthesize the complexity and tensions of various curriculum theories into frameworks for educational research.

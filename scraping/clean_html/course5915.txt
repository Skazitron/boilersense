ENGR 10400 - Introduction To Engineering And Purdue

Credit Hours: 1.00.  Weekly small group seminars led by upper-class engineering students, enabling a broader understanding of engineering disciplines at Purdue, covering skills and techniques for academic success, and offering instruction and practice in career preparation and employment finding skills. Typically offered Fall.

    1.000 Credit hours


Syllabus Available

Levels: Graduate, Professional, Undergraduate 

Schedule Types: Distance Learning, Lecture, Presentation, Presentation 1 


Offered By: First Year Engineering 

Department: Engineering Education 


Course Attributes: Lower Division 


May be offered at any of the following campuses:      

      West Lafayette


Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Successfully transition to Purdue by balancing academic and personal life.
2.  Develop life skills it takes to succeed as an engineering student.
3.  Understand the academic and career opportunities within the different fields of engineering.
4.  Develop a mentor-mentee relationship with your Peer Mentors.
5.  Develop a network of classmates with whom to study and socially interact.
6.  Learn about Purdue University resources.
7.  Learn more about the fields of engineering you want to study and with which you may work.




Restrictions:

Must be enrolled in one of the following Majors:      

      First Year Engineering

      Pre Agr & Biol Engineering

      University Division

      Explorers

VCS 80202 - Professional Skills II

Credit Hours: 2.00.  This course builds on VCS 80201 Professional Skills I. Students will be taught additional clinical skills and practice them in the clinical skills laboratory. Communication skills will be taught with the opportunity to practice them in role-plays. Financial management, professionalism and wellness topics will also be discussed. Students will participate in the care of teaching animals. Typically offered Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Demonstrate the core communication skills and apply them in a client encounter.
2.  Discuss finances with a client.
3.  Perform a neurological examination in dogs and horses, identify abnormalities and explain lesion localization.
4.  Practice a variety of clinical techniques in large and small animals such as intravenous catheter placement, endotracheal intubation, urinary catheterization, biopsy techniques.
5.  Practice suturing and instrument use.
6.  Explain the factors impacting their credit score.
7.  Explain the terms and principles used in personal finance.
8.  Understand the federal loan repayment options.
9.  Implement strategies for improving communications among peers.
10.  Explain the basic principles of radiology and be able to correctly position a patient for abdominal or thoracic radiographs.

PUBH 23000 - Community, Culture And Social Justice From A Public Health Perspective

Credit Hours: 3.00.  The course provides an introduction to the concepts of community, culture, and social justice. The interaction between community, cultural, ethical, social, economic, environmental, political and social justice dynamics that shape health will be explored.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Identify and understand the social determinants of health.
2.  Describe how community, cultural, ethical, social, economical, environmental, political and social justice shape human health both locally and globally using a social determinants of health framework.
3.  Learn about theories of justice related to the field of public health.
4.  Understand community developmental theoretical frameworks dimensions associated with the social change, such as empowerment, community participation, and capacity building.
5.  Identify roles and responsibilities of the public health educator, other practitioners, and community members as agents of change.
6.  Compare and contrast the concepts of health inequities, health disparities and health inequalities.

NUR 53900 - Psychiatric Mental Health Nurse Practitioner Roles And Therapeutic Modalities Preceptorship

Credit Hours: 2.00.  Psychotherapeutic frameworks and modalities are analyzed within the role of the PMHNP. Students participate in the application of individual and group psychotherapeutic interventions in a clinical setting by a PMHNP or therapist/clinical social worker. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Demonstrate competency in two forms of psychotherapeutic interventions.
2.  Evaluate and interpret rationale, implementation and effectiveness of psychotherapeutic interventions in a clinical setting.
3.  Implement interdisciplinary collaboration and PMHNP scope and standards of practice to provide safe, evidence-based treatment to children, adolescents, adults and older adults.
4.  Address the uniqueness of diverse populations in providing safe, evidence-based psychiatric treatment to children, adolescents, adults and older adults.
5.  Use legal and ethical standards of practice in the management of psychiatric disorders in children, adolescents, adults and older adults.
6.  Communicate orally and in writing application of PMHNP scope and standards of practice in a clinical setting.

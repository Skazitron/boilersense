NRES 33700 - Environmental Hydrology

Credit Hours: 3.00.  (AGRY 33700) This course is designed to provide undergraduate students with both the basics of how water moves through the environment and current theories as to how hydrologic response is modified by environmental change at a variety of temporal and spatial scales.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Become familiar with hydrological processes, interactions and their representations over scales from point to "continental" and times from seconds to years. The major emphasis is on surface and near surface phenomenon.
2.  Gain qualitative and quantitative understanding of land surface water budgets and how they respond to environmental change.
3.  Develop problem solving abilities on topics related to surface water hydrology.

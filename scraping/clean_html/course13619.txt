EDPS 66300 - Organization And Administration Of Special Education

Credit Hours: 3.00.  (EDST 66300) A comprehensive course in the organization and administration of special education including the organizational arrangements, identification, placement and service delivery, tests and assessments, program evaluation, personnel, and school/community relations.  Typically offered Spring.

    3.000 Credit hours

      West Lafayette


Repeatable for Additional Credit: Yes - May be repeated an unlimited number of times


Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Examine, discuss, and gain an understanding of the role of the special education director.
2.  Apply the federal and state rules and regulations for special education in school settings.
3.  Understand the role of litigation in the field of special education.
4.  Understand special education funding options, identify budget preparation strategies and fund management.
5.  Demonstrate ways to ensure parent involvement.
6.  Discuss current trends and issues in educating children with disabilities, including ethnic and cultural diversity, discipline, suspension and expulsion.

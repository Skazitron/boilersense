VM 45800 - Advanced Surgical Nursing And Anesthesia

Credit Hours: 1.00.  This course is an in-depth look at small animal surgical nursing, surgical procedures, surgical instrumentation, wound management and the physiological reason certain anesthetic protocols are used. Students will learn using a variety of teaching modalities.  Typically offered Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Identify and explain the physical exam findings and physiological changes of a patient that is a possible surgical case.
2.  Explain the physiological reasons and pharmacological impact for anesthetic protocol choices for the critical patient.
3.  Maintain and troubleshoot surgical instrumentation.
4.  Explain different pain management including epidurals, ring blocks and brachial plexus blocks.
5.  Explain the additional critical surgical nursing components that are required for the neonatal and critical care surgical patient.
6.  Explain wound management and identify the nursing skills required to care for the patient with the problematic wound.

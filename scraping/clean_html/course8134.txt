HK 32500 - Instructional Skills For Kinesiology

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Some form of instruction is part of most careers in kinesiology. In this course, students will learn about and practice using a variety of instructional skills within different movement and classroom settings. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Describe the important link between planning and instruction and explain why planning is so important to successful instructional sessions.
2.  Write behavioral goals and objectives.
3.  Create a positive learning environment.
4.  Plan and implement instructional sessions using various sources.
5.  Describe, analyze, and demonstrate effective task presentations.
6. Provide accurate and appropriate feedback to learners during and after movement attempts; analyze the various types of feedback.
7.  Use the cycle of reflection to improve his/her instructional skills and to determine the impact of his/her teaching on student learning.
8.  Choose and implement appropriate assessment techniques, including being able to write and use rubrics.

ENGL 30100 - Ways Of Reading

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Introduction to literary theory and practice. Close reading of and significant writing about selected literary texts informed by a variety of critical and/or theoretical perspectives. Typically offered Fall Spring.

    3.000 Credit hours


Syllabus Available

Levels: Graduate, Professional, Undergraduate 

Schedule Types: Distance Learning, Lecture 


Offered By: College of Liberal Arts 

Department: English 


Course Attributes: Upper Division 


May be offered at any of the following campuses:      

      West Lafayette


Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Identify various theoretical approaches to literature, and then apply them to literary texts.
2.  Define important literary terms, genres, and conventions within their historical, cultural, and aesthetic contexts.
3.  Produce well-reasoned arguments about literary texts based on appropriately selected, evaluated, and presented textual evidence.

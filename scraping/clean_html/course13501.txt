EDPS 42100 - Supporting Exceptional Learners: Social Studies And Science

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Teaching students with special needs requires the ability to locate evidence-based interventions, implement those interventions with fidelity, and track student performance over time. Mathematics is a content area in which success or failure yields major ramification for postsecondary success. This course will cover the common math interventions employed for mild to severe disabilities across grade levels K-12. This course includes assignments that will be linked to EDPS 46100 the Special Education Student Teaching Residency. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Use knowledge of general and specialized social studies curricula to individualize learning for individuals with exceptionalities.
2.  Use knowledge of general and specialized science curricula to individualize learning for individuals with exceptionalities.
3.  Develop and implement a variety of education and transition plans for individuals with exceptionalities across a wide range of academic settings.
4.  Use strategies to enhance language development and communication skills of individuals with exceptionalities across social studies and science content area.

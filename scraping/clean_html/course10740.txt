LATN 34600 - Roman Rhetoric

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Development of advanced Latin reading skills, alongside exploration of the social, political and literary questions raised by Roman rhetorical theory.  Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

    3.000 Credit hours


Syllabus Available

Department: School of Languages & Cultures

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Develop more advanced skills as readers and translators of Latin literature.
2.  Analyze Roman perspectives on communication and will discover how influential thinkers assessed the role of literature and speech in Roman society. 




Prerequisites: 

 Undergraduate level  LATN 20200 Minimum Grade of D- or Undergraduate level  CLAS L2500 Minimum Grade of D-

EDCI 53900 - Introduction To K-12 Integrated Science, Technology, Engineering And Mathematics (STEM) Education

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course will provide students with a conceptual understanding of integrated K-12 Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics (STEM) education and the nature of the disciplines of STEM. Students will explore implications for the teaching and learning of integrated STEM in a K-12 context through an evaluation of integrated approaches to STEM, national teaching standards and current research. Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Explore the nature of the disciplines of STEM and their roles in a K-12 integrated STEM context.
2.  Identify current approaches to integrated STEM education.
3.  Explore issues related to diversity and equity in STEM education.
4.  Define and describe how reform-based teaching and learning relates to nature of the disciplines of STEM.
5.  Summarize the current research on teaching and learning through integrated STEM approaches.
6.  Develop STEM integration activities for K-12 settings.
7.  Develop an action research proposal to study a problem of interest in teaching or learning in K-12 STEM settings.

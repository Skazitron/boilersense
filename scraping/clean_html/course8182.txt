HK 54100 - Athletic Training Practicum I

Credit Hours: 2.00.  Fieldwork experiences designed to integrate acquired knowledge and skills through live or scenario based applications. Students will gain clinical experiences under the direct supervision of an athletic training preceptor either on or off campus. Emphasis will be placed on professional and ethical conduct, patient centered care, interprofessional collaborative practice, evidence-based practice, quality improvement, and health care informatics. Permission of department required. Typically offered Fall.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Demonstrate active listening skills and a positive attitude when receiving constructive feedback on performance.
2.  Identify own strengths and weaknesses in professional behavior.
3.  Modify behavior based on self-evaluation and feedback from others.
4.  Demonstrate active listening, neutral body language, and sensitivity to difference in age, gender, and culture.
5.  Demonstrate flexibility and timely completion of tasks.
6.  Write legibly, using correct grammar, terminology, and spelling.
7.  Arrive punctually and dresses appropriately for clinic activities.
8.   Practice in a safe manner that minimizes risk to patient, self, and others.
9.  Demonstrate professional behavior at all times.
10.  Practice in a manner consistent with established legal and professional standards and ethical guidelines.
11.  Communicate in ways that are congruent with situational needs.
12.  Adapt delivery of athletic training services with consideration for patients’ differences, values, preferences, and needs.
13.  Participate in self-assessment to improve clinical and professional performance.
14.  Appliy current knowledge, theory, clinical judgement, and the patient’s values and perspective in patient management.
15.  Demonstrate the patient’s need for further examination or consultation by an athletic trainer or referral to another health care professional.
16.  Fit and apply prophylactic protective equipment to a patient, such as shoulder pads, helmet, custom orthoplast splints, etc in accordance with established manufacturers’ guidelines to prevent injury.
17.  Fit and apply standard protective equipment to a patient, such as crutches, cane, pneumatic walking boot, ankle braces, etc in accordance with established manufacturers’ guidelines following an injury.
18.  Apply upper and lower extremity taping and wrapping procedures consistent with biomechanical principles and appropriate for prophylactic treatment or in accordance with stage of healing.
19.  Demonstrate the ability to evaluate a patient with an emergency injury or condition.

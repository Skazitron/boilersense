CNIT 30800 - Game Development II: Design And Psychology

Credit Hours: 3.00.  (CGT 25500) This course examines video game design, theory, and development from aesthetic, psychological, and technical perspectives. Students will gain experience with a commercial game development platform. Advanced game development techniques will be taught in this course.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Deconstruct and analyze an existing game and define its essential elements as per various theories of game design.
2.  Identify major psychological paradigms and how they are applied in video game experiences.
3.  Demonstrate proficiency in applying behavior modification principles in video games.
4.  Demonstrate individual technical proficiency with a commercial game engine.
5.  Complete a market-ready video game in a team-based production environment.
6.  Implement agile production techniques.

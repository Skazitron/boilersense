MET 24500 - Manufacturing Systems

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course surveys the manufacturing processes and tools commonly used to convert cast and molded, formed, and joined materials into finished products.  It includes the fundamentals of material removal, measurement, statistical quality control, assembly processes, process planning and optimization, CNC programming and automated manufacturing. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Select and use appropriate measurement instruments and quality control methods, interpret their results, recognize potential issues and resolve them.
2.  Select appropriate machining operations, appropriate tooling, and determine basic process parameters.
3.  Recognize basic features of a manufacturing system and methods for its improvement towards cellular manufacturing.

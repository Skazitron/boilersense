ENGL 41900 - Multimedia Writing

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Multimedia writing for networked contexts.  Emphasizes principles, and practices of multimedia design, implementation, and publishing.  Typical genres include Web sites, interactive media, digital video, visual presentations, visual argument, and user documentation. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Identify the best medium for a particular rhetorical situation/audience, and articulate a justification for that choice to
stakeholders.
2.  Develop working proficiency with a suite of creative tools, including Adobe Photoshop, InDesign, and Premiere.
3.  Apply methods for user-centered design to projects; Document your design process for stakeholders, later reference, and reflection; problem solve for self-directed teaching when faced with an unfamiliar genre or tool.

PSY 35600 - Social Image And Self-Identity

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course focuses on understanding current theory and research dealing with self-identity and social media. Topics include: what constitutes the self, how the self motivates behavior (inter- and intra-personal), if what we know about ourselves is accurate, how the self develops, sources of self-esteem, our motivations related to self-esteem, how we regulate our self, how culture influences the self, the manner in which we present the self, the influence of social contexts on the self, the role romantic relationships play in shaping the self, and the influence of death on the self. Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Describe the research process as it applies to understanding the self.
2.  Demonstrate oral communication skills as a means of conveying their ideas about research that addresses self-related issues.
3.  Accurately interpret findings from research on the self and describe how such work can be applied to everyday experiences.

PTGS 23500 - Luso-Brazilian Literature In Translation

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Reading and discussion of selected masterpieces of Brazilian and Portuguese literature, with an emphasis on transatlantic cultural traditions and creative responses to contemporary social challenges.  Knowledge of Portuguese not required. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Identify and explain exemplary texts from Brazilian and Portuguese literature, by reading them and discussing them with classmates and the professor.
2.  Identify significant aspects of Luso-Brazilian culture, through discussions of examples found in assigned readings.
3.  Explain how challenges relating to racial relations, social class and everyday ethics may be addressed in different ways by another culture, through comparing their own approaches with those found in the texts.
4.  Discuss how literature relates to culture and to the analysis of social challenges, because of identifying societal implications of numerous readings.
5.  Explain the importance of satire as a coping mechanism and corrective for social problems, by commenting on assigned texts in a personal journal.
6.  Discuss how literature relates to culture and to the analysis of social challenges, through identifying societal implication of numerous readings.
7.  Write persuasively about significant aspects of colonial and post-colonial relationships, by composing and revising essays about literary texts.

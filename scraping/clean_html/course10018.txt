HSCI 33500 - Heart Physiology And Medicine

Credit Hours: 2.00.  This course will provide a discussion about diseases affecting the heart and cardiovascular system. The normal anatomy and physiology of the heart and blood vessels with be discussed. Various diseases of the heart and vasculature, including atherosclerosis, cardiomyopathies, valvular heart disease, hypertension, and stroke will be explored. Included in the discussion of each disease process will be environmental agents, which are associated with disease development and progression.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Describe the normal anatomy of the hear.
2.  Discuss the cardiac steps of the cardiac cycle.
3.  Describe disease processes of the heart and blood vessels.
4.  Identify environmental exposures associated with an increased risk of heart disease.

BME 68300 - Polymers In Biomedical And Pharmaceutical Systems

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course is designed to provide backgrounds in basic polymer chemistry, synthesis, characterization, solution properties, and recent advances in polymers for applications in drug delivery, biomedical devices, tissue engineering, biotechnology, and nanotechnology.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Understand polymer synthesis and characterization techniques.
2.  Utilize Flory-Huggins theory of solutions to describe the polymer free entropy and enthalpy.
3.  Use thermodynamics to describe the design of smart polymer systems.
4.  Describe the properties that make a material biocompatible.
5.  Understand how to design drug delivery systems.
6.  Design experiments to characterize nano-scale polymeric platforms.

HIST 31205 - The Arab-Israeli Conflict

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This class traces the emergence of political fault lines in Ottoman Palestine, the immigration of European Jews in the Interwar Era and after World War II, the divisive policies of the British Mandate, the establishment of a Jewish state and the subsequent wars between Israel and surrounding Arab countries. In the end, students study religion as one part of a complex struggle for control over regional resources. Typically offered Fall Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Increase understandings of the political, economic and cultural forces that acted as a catalyst for the Arab-Israeli conflict and continue to underpin and stir them.
2.  Reflect on the symbols (monuments, songs, literature, film, language) of Israeli and Palestinian nationalism in order to understand culture productions within conflict.
3.  Assess narratives of the Arab-Israeli conflict in film, memoir and other documents in order to develop the skills necessary to read and argue about the past, particularly in a conflict in which historical interpretation itself can be skewed due to political aims.
4.  Improve written and oral communication skills while improving analytical skills pertaining to complex issues.
5. Increase global fluency, or student knowledge about diverse cultures.

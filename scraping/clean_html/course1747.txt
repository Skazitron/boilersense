BCM 28500 - Construction Mechanics

Credit Hours: 4.00.  Principles of statics and strength of materials including properties of materials, forces, equilibrium, stresses, and strains are studied. Emphasis is placed on understanding the behavior of structural components associated with the construction process.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Understand the importance of equilibrium and the free-body concepts.
2.  Understand the nature of loads and forces acting on a structure.
3.  Develop understanding of fundamentals of structural behavior under the loading.
4.  Develop skills in computing stress and strain in a structural member and to recognize their importance in design and construction.
5.  Develop a logical and systematic approach to problem solving and an appreciation of values of working in a neat and orderly fashion.

VCS 24000 - Nutrition For Veterinary Technicians - DL

Credit Hours: 1.00.  This course will provide an overview of nutritional concepts as they apply to common domestic small and large animal species and selected exotic companion/avian pets. Basic concepts for understanding nutritional needs for all species will be introduced as well as the therapeutic use of diets as they relate to animal nursing. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Assess normal nutritional requirements of the animal (ruminant vs. monogastric) as they apply to physiological processes.
2.  Explain BCS evaluation as it relates to obesity, weight loss, and weight management programs.
3.  Identify adult, pediatric and geriatric nutritional needs.
4.  Describe the changing nutritional needs from pediataric stages to adulthood.
5.  Define specific differences in diets based upon breeds (large vs. small, selected breed well dog needs).
6.  Compare, contrast and analyze food labels.
7.  Evaluate diets and dietary supplements, nutraceuticals, hay, feed and pasture management.
8.  Plan and perform nutritional counseling/weight management programs by educating clients on dietary needs of pets and livestock, incorporating the roles of the technician and veterinarian.

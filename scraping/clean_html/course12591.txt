IET 33100 - Advanced Industrial Safety And Health Management

Credit Hours: 3.00.  An introduction to OSHA and standards development for occupational health in general industry.  Special emphasis is on fire protection and egress, flammable and combustible liquids, electrical, personal protective equipment, machine guarding, industrial hygiene/blood borne pathogens, ergonomics, and ISO 9000/14000 integration. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Explain the purpose of the Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA).
2.  Describe the fundamental requirements of each of the General Industry Regulations in 29 CFR 1910.
3.  Apply the General Industry Regulations in real-life scenarios within the workplace.
4.  Explain how to prepare for the OHSA workplace inspection.
5.  Create an adequate training plan for workplace employees.
6.  Identify readily available resources for creating a workplace free from recognized hazards.

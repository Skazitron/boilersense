EDCI 57500 - Foundations Of Distance Learning

Credit Hours: 3.00.  An introduction to the field of distance learning/education.  Examination of  basic concepts and principles of distance learning, the theoretical underpinnings of the field, research and application literature, and distance education delivery technologies. Focus is on integration of distance education technologies for learning and teaching. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Discuss common distance education (DE) definitions and theories.
2.  Understand how distance education has evolved including the history, research and current trends of DE. 
3.  Select and demonstrate appropriate technologies for use in DE environments.
4.  Identify appropriate instructional design methods.  
5.  Apply appropriate instructional design methods to DE scenarios.
6.  Demonstrate understanding of unique challenges of teaching at a distance.  
7.  Appreciate common assessment and evaluation methods and incorporate them into DE scenarios. 
8.  Discuss potential issues and barriers faced by learners and instructors in DE environments. 
9.  Be aware of and discuss policies that relate to DE. 
10.  Be aware of current trends in the field of distance education and how they relate to your focus.

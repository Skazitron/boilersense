SFT 36100 - Space Security, Law, Policy, And Doctrine II

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course address the use of space power as an instrument of national power, emphasizing Space Force doctrine, joint doctrine, and space as an operational domain of war. Students examine both the United States' and other nations' space security programs and how international partners and adversaries view and apply space power.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Explain the application of space power through unified space action.
2.  Compare and contrast Space Force doctrine and joint doctrine.
3.  Compare and contrast space law, policy, and doctrine in historical contexts of space security.
4.  Explain how Space Force core competencies support national policy.

PSY 22200 - Introduction To Behavioral Neuroscience

Credit Hours: 3.00.  An introduction to how the nervous system controls behavior. Topics include evolution and comparative psychobiology, the neuroscience of sensation and perception, the neuroscience of motivation, neuropsychology, and cognitive neuroscience. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Describe the basic structure and function of the brain including neuroanatomy and neural communication.
2.  Explain how the central nervous system processes internal and external stimuli.
3.  Explain how experience influences current and future behavior.
4.  Discern normal from abnormal brain function and describe its influence on behavior.

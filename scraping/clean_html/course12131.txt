EDCI 45200 - Global Studies Seminar

Credit Hours: 1.00.  (EDPS 45200) The course builds on students' international cross-cultural experiences, particularly Study Abroad. The course is required for those wishing to complete the Global Studies minor in the College of Education. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

    1.000 Credit hours

      West Lafayette


Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Discuss economic, social, and cultural diversity within a global context.
2.  Describe the cultural, social and historical dynamics that influence individuals and groups.
3.  Explain the perspective of the culture of another country through the study of world languages, arts, spiritual traditions, mythology/literature, and/or through study abroad.
4.  Identify examples of how social, behavioral, or historical knowledge informs and can shape personal, civic, ethical, or global decisions and responsibilities.
5.  Clearly communicate a central message with the supporting materials.

ECON 63100 - Industrial Organization

Credit Hours: 1.00 to 3.00.  A study of asset selection within the expected utility approach, with some attention given to the non-expected utility decision models. Topics covered include axioms for measurable utility, monetary models under uncertainty, models of capital budgets, and security-selection models. Prerequisite: Master's student standing or higher.

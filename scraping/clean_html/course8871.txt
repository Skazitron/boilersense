ECON 67800 - Search Theory And Applications

Credit Hours: 2.00 or 3.00.  This course provides an overview to the tools and applications of search theory, which has been a very active area of research. The aim of this course is to provide students with a solid understanding of the search approach and to get started on engaging in research. Prerequisite: ECON 60800 or ECON 61100 or ECON 61200.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Formulate and solve general equilibrium search models.
2.  Use search models to interpret empirical regularities and assess policy.
3.  Critically discuss and evaluate papers in the literature.
4.  Identify topics for future research that can be studied with the tools of search theory.

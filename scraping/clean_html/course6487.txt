CSR 12800 - Introduction To Apparel Design

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course introduces the fundamentals of the Apparel industry, design, and assembly through lectures, discussions, readings and application in an Apparel laboratory. The student will learn the proper operation of industrial garment production equipment. Further, the course will introduce the manufacturing standards of apparel assembly through garment construction, principles of fit and fiber, and fabric performance.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Explain the basics of the nature, materials, producers, and markets of and for fashion and the apparel industry.
2.  Identify basic apparel assembly and design techniques, principles of fit, and fiber and fabric performance characteristics within specific types of garments.
3.  Operate garment production equipment in a safe and appropriate manner.

HDFS 69700 - Gradate Student Colloquium

Credit Hours: 1.00.  Research presentations by faculty members and graduate students and invited speakers.  Includes attention to: (1) the responsible conduct of research (2) professional/ scholarly and teaching development. (3) diversity, equity, and inclusion.

    1.000 Credit hours


Syllabus Available

Levels: Graduate, Professional, Undergraduate 

Schedule Types: Distance Learning, Lecture 


Offered By: College of Health & Human Sci 

Department: Human Dev and Family Studies 



May be offered at any of the following campuses:      

      West Lafayette


Repeatable for Additional Credit: Yes - May be repeated up to 13 times


Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Expose students to a wide swath of HDFS-related research across the life course.
2.  Provide a rotation of professional development trainings (e. g., vita, job talk, and conference presentation development).
3.  Foster teaching development through presentations, workshops, or trainings.
4.  Generate internal and external networking opportunities for graduate students.
5.  Address issues of diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI) in HDFS by (a) providing a platform for increased representation in presenters, (b) critically considering how research in HDFS serves diverse (i. e., underrepresented/underserved/historically marginalized) populations, and (c) increasing our competencies related to DEI as part of professional development.


Restrictions:

Must be enrolled in one of the following Levels:      

      Graduate

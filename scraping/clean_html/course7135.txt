HTM 50300 - Business Statistics And Quantitative Analysis In Hospitality

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Data analysis is an important skill for effective managerial decision making.  As advances in technology have made substantial amounts of data available to managers, it is increasingly important to be able to convert business data into actionable information. This course deals with the fundamentals of statistical analysis and forecasting techniques. Students will develop the ability to summarize and quantitatively analyze business data and make forecasts while taking into account seasonality and trends. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Graphically and numerically summarize data.
2.  Make inferences about population means and variances.
3.  Compute statistics from sample data to support confidence interval estimation, hypothesis testing, and regression analysis.
4.  Conduct chi-square tests and t-tests and interpret the results.
5.  Conduct ANOVA (Analysis of Variance) tests and interpret the results.
6.  Conduct regression analysis and interpret the results.
7.  Apply various methods of forecasting.
8.  Recognize and mange seasonal patterns and trends in data.
9.  Conduct statistical analysis and forecasting techniques in Excel.

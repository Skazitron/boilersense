EDPS 63600 - Item Response Theory

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Models and model-data fit, estimation of ability and item parameters, item and test information, test construction, obtaining evidence of test reliability and validity, differential item functioning, norming, and equating.   Prerequisite:  EDPS 53100, (PSY 60100 or STAT 50200 or 51200). Typically offered Summer Fall Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Explain fundamental item response theory concepts and terminology.
2.  Distinguish and select from among 1-, 2-, and 3- parameter item response models for dichotomous data in practical situations.
3.  Demonstrate familiarity with the graded response and partial credit item response models for polytomous data, and multidimensional item response models.
4.  Use item response models for testing applications, such as item calibration during instrument/item pool development, estimation of person parameters and conditional standard errors of measurement, investigation of differential item functions, and score linking.
5.  Conduct item response modeling with specialized software and interpret the results.

ENGL 33000 - Games And Diversity

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course looks critically at diversity in games, game development, and in the larger mainstream games community. Students will analyze and play games that related to women, minorities, members of the LGBTQ community, and/or those who lack access because of disability. Typically offered Fall Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Analyze a game's narrative based on a specific frame of diversity (i.e. gender, ethnicity, sexuality, and/or disability).
2.  Explain how video games relate to contemporary cultural issues.
3.  Use evidence-based arguments to defend a position related to video games and diversity.

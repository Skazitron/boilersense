SPAN 41500 - Spanish Translation And Interpreting

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Introduction to the Translation and Interpretation Studies field for Spanish speakers.  High level of competence in Spanish required.  Overview of translation theory, methodology, and practice both in class and in real settings.  Includes a service learning component.  Conducted in English and Spanish.  Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Have an overview of the translation studies field in its relation to Linguistics, Literature, Media, Sciences, and Second Language Acquisition.
2.  Understand the different methodologies used for translation and interpretation.
3.  Learn the ethics, standards and rules of the profession.
4.  Practice different types of translation (technical, scientific, literary, legal medical, etc.) and interpretation (simultaneous, consecutive, over the phone, conference, etc.).
5.  Understand the basic principles of cross-cultural communication.

EDCI 49601 - Elementary Education Student Teaching Residency

Credit Hours: 1.00 to 8.00.  This course is the initial residency experience that precedes a full semester of student teaching. The seminar and field experience focus on induction into the broader elementary school context and building mentoring relationships as both a mentee and mentor. Students will develop their identity as a professional educator who advocates for the profession, themselves, and their students. Includes a field experience. Admission to Teacher Education required. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Integrate their learning from Foundations and Methods courses into their teaching experiences in the classroom.
2.  Design and deliver instruction that meets learner needs, continually assess student learning, and use assessment to drive instruction.
3.  Develop their skills and professional identity as an educator through self-reflection, analysis of student learning, and analysis of feedback.
4.  Use observation, academic and personal engagement, and assessment data to develop an understanding of their students as learners at the individual and group level.

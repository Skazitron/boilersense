HTM 53100 - Hospitality And Tourism Marketing II

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course will examine marketing strategy in tourism and hospitality organizations. Students will actively engage in the application, analysis, and evaluation of marketing principles as they are applied to tourism and hospitality. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Define marketing strategy and describe its use in the hospitality industry.
2.  Detail the components of marketing strategy and marketing plan for a hospitality and tourism organization.
3.  Evaluate the strengths and weaknesses of hospitality or tourism organization's marketing strategy and plan.
4.  Evaluate metrics designed to measure marketing strategy effectiveness.
5.  Explain the major market trends in the hospitality industry.
6.  Distinguish between major marketing research techniques and explain their advantages and disadvantages.

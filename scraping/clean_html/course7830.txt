HDFS 45100 - Internship & Career Preparation For Human Services

Credit Hours: 2.00.  This course includes an overview of HDFS-related professions, self-evaluation, career search skills, exploration of internship opportunities, and professional development. Students will complete arrangements for their field placement.

    2.000 Credit hours

Department: Human Dev and Family Studies 


Course Attributes: Upper Division 


May be offered at any of the following campuses:      

      West Lafayette


Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Assess career interests and identify potential internship sites.
2.  Create a professional cover letter and resume.
3.  Identify how resources available at the Purdue Center for Career Opportunities (CCO) and its website (myCCO) can be used in the job/internship search.
4.  Develop skills and strategies for conducting an effective job/internship.
5.  Develop and apply professional interview skills.
6.  Demonstrate professional behavior through the successful and timely completion of course assignments.
7.  Demonstrate preparation for the transition from student to intern.

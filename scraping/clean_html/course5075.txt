ECE 30411 - Electromagnetics I

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course is a continued study of vector calculus, electrostatics, magnetostatics, and Maxwell's Equations. It serves as an introduction to electromagnetic waves and transmission lines, which is continued in ECE 30412.

    3.000 Credit hours

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Work with electrostatic fields and find electric and potential fields from charge distributions including the presence of dielectric materials.
2.  Work with magnetostatic fields and find magnetic fields from current distributions including the presence of magnetic materials.
3.  Work with time varying fields including wave propagation.
4.  Work with transmission lines in the time and frequency domains.

LC 23100 - Fairytale, Folktale, Fable

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course provides an overview of fairytale, folklore, and fable traditions across various cultures and historical periods, as well as an examination of their ongoing influence on contemporary cultures.  Taught in English.  Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Gain an intercultural knowledge of human cultures as well as global citizenship and awareness.
2.  Make comparisons and connections about the interplay and the importance of such traditional tales among various cultures of the world.
3.  Enable students to develop both their written communication and their oral communication skills.
4.  Develop critical thinking, creative thinking, and in some instances, ethical reasoning.
5.  Develop information literacy skills and the integrative knowledge, not only through classwork, but also through assignments and projects.

HTM 10010 - Introduction To The Hospitality And Tourism Industry

Credit Hours: 1.00.  This course gives students an opportunity in one of the world's top ranked programs to accompany award-winning professors in an exploration of the dynamic and evolving world of people management, also known as the hospitality and tourism industry; one of the largest industries in the world.  This course describes the varied market segment and vast opportunities that exist for our graduates in areas such as hotels, restaurants, even planning, tourism, and healthcare, just to name a few.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Describe market segments in the hospitality and tourism industries and their impact on the global economy.
2.  Describe how hospitality is the difference that defines the industry.
3.  Describe contemporary challenges facing the hospitality and tourism industries.
4.  Demonstrate industry professionalism.
5.  Identify and describe career paths in the hospitality and tourism industries.
6.  Identify the global evolution of the hospitality and tourism industries beginning with the U.S.
7.  Describe industry relevant issues such as fast tracking, networking, work ethics, and multicultural environment as presented by industry executives.

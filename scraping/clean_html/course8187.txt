HK 55500 - Principles Of Manual Medicine

Credit Hours: 2.00.  This course will provide students with the theory, skills, and clinical application of manual medicine. The emphasis will be placed on passive and dynamic joint mobilizations, connective tissue massage, deep friction massage, lymphatic massage, myofascial release, passive range of motion exercises, muscle energy techniques, and nerve mobilization. All skills will be introduced through onsite demonstration and hands-on practice. Permission of department required. Typically offered Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Describe the basic theories and principles of various types of manual medicine.
2.  Evaluate the patient indications and contraindications, treatment efficacy, and clinical applications of each kind of manual medicine technique.
3.  Summarize and select appropriate manual medicine techniques to match injury pathophysiology.
4.  Choose and apply manual medicine treatment techniques, including passive and dynamic joint mobilizations, connective tissue massage, deep friction massage, lymphatic massage, myofascial release, passive range of motion exercises, muscle energy techniques, and nerve mobilization.
5.  Create a plan of care including manual medicine in preparation for exercise and recovery from exercise.
6.  Compare the role of manual medicine in a multi-disciplinary approach to treatment of soft tissue injuries.

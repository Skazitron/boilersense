NUR 10900 - Introduction To Nursing II

Credit Hours: 1.00.  Using knowledge acquired in NUR 10800 students will examine the profession of nursing and their role and responsibilities within the profession. Topics to be discussed include informatics and technology, the importance of professional communication in oral and written form, the global image of nursing, wellness and self-care, and preparation for progression into the sophomore year. Permission of department required. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Demonstrate the ability to communicate in oral and written form at a professional level.
2.  Explore the roles of the professional nurse in both domestic and global healthcare delivery systems.
3.  Examine the role of social media and its impact on healthcare.
4.  Identify components of effective and ineffective communication in the healthcare environment.
5.  Discuss the use of informatics in documenting the management of patient care.
6.  Identify skills and personal behaviors necessary for success in an academic and professional career.

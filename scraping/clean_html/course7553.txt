BIOL 44202 - Animal Physiology

Credit Hours: 2.00.  This is a project-oriented course designed to give the student exposure to laboratory research through a series of five-week modules. This module involves measurements of respiratory, cardiovascular, neural and renal function. When appropriate measurements are made in both rats and humans.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Conduct both animal and human physiological experiments and to learn how to deal with the complexities inherent in live animal experiments.
2.  Appreciate the advantages and limitations involved in both human and animal experiments.
3.  Learn basic surgical anatomy and surgical anatomy and surgical procedures.
4.  Gain an appreciation for the accurate measurement, analysis, evaluation and interpretation of physiological results.
5.  Gain some practice in presenting and explaining results to a group.

DANC 34500 - Choreography

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course focuses on methods and practices of choreographing contemporary dance works. It teaches the choreographic process: how to create original movement and expand and build both solo and group dance works. Basic compositional elements of exploring and manipulating movement and structuring choreographed material are utilized. The development of production elements are taught to support choreographic intent. Highly recommended before enrolling in this course: DANC 24500 and DANC 30100.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Learn the application of choreographic tools which will allow students to create a coherent dance composition. 
2.  Increased awareness for choreography and understanding its value and meaning as both a creator and observer. 
3.  Discuss and critique dance choreography while developing a sense of personal expression and artistry.

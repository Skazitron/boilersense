ENGL 60311 - Medical And Healthcare Writing

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course help students master various genres, writing styles, and rhetorical strategies common in medical and healthcare contexts. More specifically, you will learn how to analyze, prepare, and edit documents that are used to communicate within and about the healthcare industry (i.e. protocols, patient educational materials, and proposals). Permission of department required. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Identify and understand the written genres that are common in healthcare and medical fields.
2.  Analyze audiences for medical and healthcare documents and understand their needs.
3.  Generate and translate health complex medical information to both healthcare professionals and the public.
4.  Organize information in a way that is accessible and useful in healthcare workplaces. 
5.  Develop and design professional quality medical documents such as protocols, patient educational materials, and proposals.

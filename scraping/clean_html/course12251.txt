EDCI 64600 - Supervision In Career And Technical Education

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Purposes, principles, strategies, and procedures of supervision and management in career and technical education and related workforce education and development contexts: theory and practice: human resource development: environment, education, and management.  Approval of Department required.  Typically offered Fall Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Define and describe contemporary supervision concepts.
2.  Identify and explain principles of educational supervision.
3.  Relate and analyze critically emerging concepts in supervision to social, political, economic, and cultural factors.
4.  Describe and discuss a sequence of (clinical) experiences that provide optimum opportunity for developing educator competencies.
5.  Identify and apply best practices experiences that have potential for practica, internships, and other work-based learning programs.
6.  Identify and describe the stages of supervision.
7.  Identify, evaluate, select, and apply techniques and strategies appropriate to the implementation of each stage of supervision.
8.  Implement methods of supervision in pre-observation, conference, observation, analysis and strategy, conference, and post-conference contexts.
9.  Develop awareness and participate in professional development opportunities in CTE and education.

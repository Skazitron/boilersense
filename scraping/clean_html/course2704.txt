TLI 15200 - Business Principles For Organizational Leadership

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course will introduce the topic of applied organization leadership in the context of working organizations. Topics include basic functions, structures, and operations of organizations, and an introduction to reading and understanding balance sheets, cash flow statements, and profit-loss statements. Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Identify the business information that organizational leaders are expected to know to comprehensively manage the function and operation of profit and not-profit organizations.
2.  Describe the major functions of business, including: operations, finance, marketing, and information technology.
3.  Interpret balance sheets, cash flow statements, and profit-loss statements.
4.  Explore how organizations use technology to become competitive entities in global business environments.

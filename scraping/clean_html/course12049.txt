EDCI 30101 - Inquiry Into Teaching And Learning In K-2

Credit Hours: 1.00.  The goal of the seminar is to connect theory to practice by engaging in an immersive field experience that connects to content and courses taken concurrently through cycles of inquiry. In particular, this seminar is intended to be an inquiry space for questions and topics related to teaching using pedagogies of care in the K-2 classroom. Admission to Teacher Education required. Cannot be taken concurrently with EDCI 30002. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Engage with teachers and students in connected, educative, and personally rewarding field experiences.
2.  Engage in practical experiences with opportunities to observe, plan, teach, assess, and receive feedback.
3.  Gain understanding of the professional responsibilities of a teacher.
4.  Explore the role of collaboration among colleagues in a school.
5.  Draw connections and distinctions among disciplinary practices.
6.  Be active participants in an ongoing, supportive lesson inquiry group.
7.  Evaluate the outcomes of their teaching practice and constructively respond to feedback.
8.  Receive mentoring and engage in peer mentoring.
9.  Examine collaboratively and critically, educational needs within the field placement.

EDPS 62800 - The Behavior Analyst As Supervisor

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course will address the provision of supervision of behavior analysis trainees and technicians utilizing principles and procedures of applied behavior analysis. Application of goal development, behavioral skills training, and progress monitoring within a behavioral analytic framework will be explored. Must be enrolled in ABA Certificate or Masters Program. Pre-requisite: EDPS 61200. Permission of instructor required. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Describe reasons for effective evidence-based supervision and outcomes of ineffective supervision.
2.  Describe necessary components of the supervisory relationship.
3.  Assess and select performance expectations for the supervisee and supervisor.
4.  Describe components of behavioral skills training.
5.  Apply supervision skills to teach assessment and intervention procedures as relevant to task list competencies.
6.  State features of performance monitoring and performance feedback .
7.  Evaluate supervisor effectiveness.
8.  Describe ethics of and methods for providing ongoing professional development.

HTM 57100 - Economics Of Travel And Tourism

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course includes the asset theory of tourism, cost-benefit analysis, tax policy impacts, and other economic and statistical aspects of tourism. The course will examine research designed to determine economic impacts of the hospitality and tourism industries. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Acquire an understanding of the economic aspects of tourism, including the definitions of tourism and the size, persuasiveness, and data available on the industry, both internationally and domestically.
2.  Develop an understanding of the importance and impacts of tourism and the marketing segmentation, supply, demand, and monetary exchange rates that impact the economies of tourism destinations.
3.  Develop working knowledge of the various issues, problems, forecasts and types of research common in the tourism industry to include economic impact analysis such as input output analysis and multipliers, forecasting models and other tools used to analyze the tourism industry.

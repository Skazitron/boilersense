ECET 31410 - Military RF Electronic Applications

Credit Hours: 3.00.  The theory and techniques of using RF transmissions to support military operations are studied from a systems point of view.  This includes functions such as identification, location and tracking, communications, jamming, and deception.  Many of the technologies studied are also widely used in civilian applications, such as air traffic control, satellite communications, navigation, and homeland defense.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Apply electronic warfare (EW) techniques.
2.  Analyze/design antenna systems.
3.  Analyze RF propagation characteristics under various conditions.
4.  Describe the function and operation of a range of military systems.
5.  Apply a variety of EW techniques and characterize their effectiveness in different operating environments.

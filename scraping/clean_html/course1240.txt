IDE 48500 - Multidisciplinary Engineering Design Project

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Capstone design experience for multidisciplinary engineering students.  Physical system or process system design projects, related to contemporary or potential problems involving interdisciplinary teams of engineers. Permission of instructor required. Typically offered Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Function as an effective member of a multidisciplinary team.
2.  Demonstrate leadership in a team context.
3.  Apply a systemic approach to the early design stages of the product life cycle.
4.  Identify and verify stakeholder needs and thus develop valid design requirements.
5.  Idently competing technical, commercial, socio-environmental issues.
6. Use advanced informtion seeking and computer knowledge management tools.
7.  Synthesize a conceptual design for a complex product, machine or system.
8.  Use advanced computer tools to model and test the behaviour of design ideas.
9.  Estimate the life cycle costs of a system at the conceptual phase.
10.  Manage technical and other types of risk in early stages of the design process.
11.  Demonstrate effective project management and apply professional judgement.
12.  Conduct professional design reviews.

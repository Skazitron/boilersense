PUBH 10000 - Introduction To Public Health

Credit Hours: 1.00.  This course serves as an introduction to the field of public health. Topics include identification of the principal determinants of health and how they affect individuals and populations, the role of government and public health professionals in health and healthcare, and critical and emerging issues in public health in the US and around the world. Typically offered Fall Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Acquire a basic understanding of the field of Public Health.
2.  Identify the principal determinants of health and disease.
3.  Understand the government's role in matters pertaining to the health of the public.
4.  Explain the role of public health in medical care and identify the principal problems in the US healthcare system.
5.  Achieve a basic understanding of the two major analytical methods of public health: epidemiology and biostatistics.
6.  Identify the principal environmental determinants of health.
7.  Understand the health challenges that confront major vulnerable populations.
8.  Have knowledge of major and widespread challenges to good health: smoking, obesity, chronic disease, violence, and social and economic inequities.
9.  Recognize critical and emerging issues in global health.
10.  Describe future challenges in public health.

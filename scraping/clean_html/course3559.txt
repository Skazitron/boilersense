COM 49503 - Public Relations For Social Change

Credit Hours: 3.00.  The overarching goal of this course is for students to work cooperatively with a community partner to identify, define and address that organization's PR problems and opportunities from a long-term, in-house perspective, building on the work of previous students and incorporating social change theory from a relationship management perspective of PR. The applied work of this course will have the dual benefit of providing students the opportunity to gain contextualized, real-world experience at PR problem-solving and advancing the mission of a nonprofit organization that is important to the well-being of the Greater Lafayette community as a whole.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Construct and devise a strategic and mission-focused PR plan and critically evaluate its effects.
2.  Explain and demonstrate how storytelling and community relations PR strategies can be used as tools for social change.
3.  Describe how their own PR skillsets can be means for future civic engagement.
4.  Develop an increased sense of efficacy when it comes to matters of civic engagement and PR practice.
5.  Develop a broader view of how PR as a discipline can be applied within a community.

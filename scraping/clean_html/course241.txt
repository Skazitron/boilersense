ENGL 38000 - Issues In Rhetoric And Public Life

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Study of how language and media create belief and change. Examines ancient and contemporary rhetorical theory as it applies to public discourse, media, and technology.

    3.000 Credit hours


Syllabus Available

Levels: Graduate, Professional, Undergraduate 

Schedule Types: Distance Learning, Lecture 


Offered By: College of Liberal Arts 

Department: English 


Course Attributes: GTC-Written Communication, GTC-Information Literacy, UC-Written Communication, UC-Information Literacy, Upper Division 


May be offered at any of the following campuses:      

      West Lafayette


Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Demonstrate knowledge of a range rhetorical techniques and practices in the public realm, past and/or present.
2.  Discuss those techniques and practices in relation to their diverse historical, cultural, and technological contexts.
3.  Produce well-reasoned written arguments about the rhetorical techniques and practices they study using appropriate forms of research and evidence.

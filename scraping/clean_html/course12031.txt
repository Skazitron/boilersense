EDCI 21510 - Language & Literacy Foundations For Teachers

Credit Hours: 1.00.  The goal of the course is to provide background information in language and literacy necessary for success in literacy methods courses. The course will address basic linguistics and phonetic patterns of English, as well as the structures of literature and literacy terms. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Compare and contrast levels of language structure: phoneme, morpheme, root, affix, phrase, sentence.
2.  Differentiate graphic from phonological elements of language (e.g., grapheme vs phoneme). 
3.  Compare and contrast vocabulary terms (e.g. synonym, homonym, homograph).
4.  Define and articulate written elements (e.g. digraphs, consonant clusters, long/short vowels).
5.  Identify morpho-syntactic structures and phonological patters in written and spoken language.
6.  Compare and contrast literary genres (e.g. narrative, non-narrative, fiction, nonfiction).
7.  Distinguish the organizational structures of fictional genres from those of nonfictional genres.
8.  Identify literary terms in context (e.g. alliteration, character traits, plot, stanza).

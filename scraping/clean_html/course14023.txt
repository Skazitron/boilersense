AD 33200 - Visual Communication Design I

Credit Hours: 3.00.  A course designed to introduce the student to projects that will develop an understanding of basic problem-solving techniques in the areas of publication and promotional graphics. Acceptance into professional VCD sequence via mandatory portfolio review.

    3.000 Credit hours


Syllabus Available

Levels: Graduate, Professional, Undergraduate 

Schedule Types: Distance Learning, Studio 


Offered By: College of Liberal Arts 

Department: Patti & Rusty Rueff Schl DAP 


Course Attributes: Upper Division 


May be offered at any of the following campuses:      

      West Lafayette


Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Provide opportunities to explore the elements and principles of design and process as they relate to typography and letter forms.
2.  Engage students' conceptual skills in branding design.
3.  Further strengthen students' ability to create effective visual communications by way of complex problems emphasizing critical thinking, design process and presentation.

AT 20700 - Introduction To Aircraft Systems

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Principles and design concepts of aircraft operating systems are covered. Emphasis is given to flight control, electrical, environmental, hydraulic, pneumatic, pressurization, oxygen, heating, cooling, fire protection, and ice and rain protection systems. Also included are theory of operation and design of aircraft instruments and associate systems.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Learn the function and operational principles aircraft instruments.
2.  Learn the function of various components in a system for maintaining a healthful and comfortable environment in an aircraft.
3.  Study, inspect, and check ice, rain, and fire control aircraft systems.
4.  Determine the location of the elements in the aircraft and become familiar with the function and operating procedures for the typical system found on light single or light twin engine aircraft.
5.  Learn to recognize various fittings and their application when used for rigid tubing and flexible hose.
6.  Learn the use of tools and FAA Regulations in relation to aircraft tube and hose fabrication.
7.  Learn the basic fundamentals of hydraulic systems, power, components and control. 
8.  Learn the different hydraulic fluids, seal design and system compatibility used in hydraulic systems.

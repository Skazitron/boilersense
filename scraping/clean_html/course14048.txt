AD 34700 - Lighting For Interior Environments

Credit Hours: 3.00.  The study of illuminating principles, design criteria, specifications and environmental systems applied to architectural interiors in public and private spaces. Permission of department required.

    3.000 Credit hours

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Know sustainable concepts and the environmental impact of illumination strategies and decisions.
2.  Know a range of sources for information and research about light and color.
3.  Understand the principles of natural and artificial lighting design.
4.  Understand light and color terminology, principles, theories, and techniques.
5.  Understand how light and color in the interior environment impact health, safety, and wellbeing.
6.  Demonstrate appropriate use of strategies for using and modulating natural and artificial light.
7.  Demonstrate appropriate use of types, benefits, and application of luminaires and light sources in terms of function, aesthetics, and accessible design.
8.  Demonstrate appropriate use of color in relation to materials, textures, light, and form.
9.  Demonstrate appropriate use color and lighting solutions across different modes of design communication.

MET 30200 - CAD In The Enterprise

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Theory and practice of management, use and integration of computer-aided design systems, and related engineering tools and practices are studied as they are applied in the industrial enterprise. Emphasis is on course projects. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Operate a variety of CAD software packages to create models, assemblies, and fully annotated mechanical and electrical drawings that adhere to accepted drafting standards.
2.  Appropriately apply GD&T, fits and tolerances to complex mechanical assemblies.
3.  Develop practical system designs and part designs that are capable of being manufactured, including visual, calculated, and simulated analysis to assure design validity.
4.  Transfer CAD files of various file formats to CAM software and set up simple tool path operations for machining.

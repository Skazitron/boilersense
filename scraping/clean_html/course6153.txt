VCS 24200 - Diagnostic Imaging For Veterinary Technicians - DL

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course involves principles of imaging that are necessary for the performance of diagnostic imaging procedures in a veterinary hospital. This course provides an introduction to x-ray generation, image formation, exposure factors, scatter radiation, grids, film, screens, safety, basic terminology, positioning, darkroom techniques, artifact identification, advanced positioning techniques for the small animal, large animal techniques, contrast techniques, technique charts, basic ultrasound, diagnostic imaging in exotic animals. Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Explain the basic and advanced concepts of diagnostic imaging for small and large animal patients.
2.  Describe fundamental techniques used in setting up, taking, and developing radiographs.
3.  Explain concepts of safe use of radiographic equipment.
4.  Present key points on assessment of radiographic technique.
5.  Describe techniques used in large animal, exotics, and special procedures.
6.  Explain the process required to develop a radiographic technique chart.
7.  Explain the basic information regarding ultrasound.
8.  Differentiate the uses for CT/MRI.

AD 31900 - Web Design for Visual Communication

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Introduction to web-based design from a visual communication application.

    3.000 Credit hours


Syllabus Available

Levels: Graduate, Professional, Undergraduate 

Schedule Types: Distance Learning, Studio 


Offered By: College of Liberal Arts 

Department: Patti & Rusty Rueff Schl DAP 


Course Attributes: Upper Division 


May be offered at any of the following campuses:      

      West Lafayette


Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Design desktop and mobile sites prototypes using Adobe XD, Photoshop, and Illustrator.
2.  Understand and produce responsive web design strategies.
3.  Use a workflow model to produce visual communications strategies.
4.  Conduct design research for the web.
5.  Produce flowcharts, wireframes, and progress reports.
6.  Apply graphic design strategies to web design (e.g., use of grid systems, visual hierarchy, etc.).
7.  Identify web design elements, techniques and trends.
8.  Analyze web designs based on design perspectives such as content design, graphic design, experience design, navigation design, and performance design (technical aspects).

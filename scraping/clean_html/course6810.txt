HIST 51100 - England Under The Tudors

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Examines English society from 1485 to 1603, examining the evolution of English kingship; changing religious climate; growth of parliament; and England's entry into the era of European expansionism. It is also concerned with family life, marriage, children, Renaissance and folk culture.  Permission of Instructor required.  Typically offered Fall Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Understand the history and culture of sixteenth-century England.
2.  Read and discuss key texts from sixteenth-century England.
3.  Develop critical thinking, writing and reading skills.
4.  Analyze political, economic, social, and cultural richness and diversity of their era as well as England's place within a global context.

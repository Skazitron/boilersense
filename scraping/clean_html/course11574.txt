PSY 58100 - Neuroethics

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Neuroscience research has led to a better understanding of the neuronal basis of behavior. The knowledge, together with new technological approaches that can predict and even control some aspects of human behavior, can have a major impact on social and legal policies. The course explores ethical, social, and legal implications of the use of new technological and pharmacological advances in brain research.  Permission of department required. Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Learn some of the technology and pharmocology that is currently used to study/manipulate the human mind and behavior.
2.  Critically evaluate ethical arguments/dilemmas surrounding the use of such scientific advances.
3.  Research in depth a real-world topic in Neuroethics that may have social and/or legal implications at the present time.

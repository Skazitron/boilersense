HTM 53600 - Advanced Service Management in Hospitality And Tourism

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Approximately two-thirds of U.S. economic activity resides in the service sector. Subsequently, service firms' foci on the needs of their customers are rewarded by positive customer outcomes such as behavioral loyalty and positive word of mouth. This course explores and informs the design, management, and innovative service strategies of firms who operate in the service sector through the theoretical and empirical services management literature. Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Describe and apply basic service management principles.
2.  Understand how to determine customer needs and simultaneously manage business performance.
3.  Create a service blueprint and use it to inform service design or redesign.
4.  Identify excellence in service from a variety of sectors across the globe.
5.  Describe and apply basic service management principles.
6.  Distinguish service management from customer service principles.
7.  Perform a service audit for an organization.
8.  Design sustainable service strategies and approaches.
9.  Understand the mechanisms necessary in developing a service culture.
10.  Craft the necessary elements in funding service excellence.

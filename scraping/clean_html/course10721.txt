ENTR 47000 - Gender, Diversity And Leadership

Credit Hours: 3.00.  The course exposes students to the historical, social, cultural and organizational dimensions of gender and leadership. Through research and current events, students become aware of the challenges and opportunities facing individuals who wish to work in historically male-dominated fields and/or who aspire to leadership positions. Students develop self-awareness and practical skills to enhance their leadership abilities through activities and interaction with leaders from a variety of professions and organizations. They enhance the professional, communication and negotiation skills necessary for leadership success. ENTR 47000 serves as an option course for the Certificate in Entrepreneurship and Innovation.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Understand the historical, cultural and organizational dimensions of gender and leadership.
2.  Identify the unique challenges women and minorities face within the workplace.
3.  Enhance sensitivity to bias and gender schemas and their influence.
4.  Develop strategies to overcome the implicit and explicit barriers.
5.  Practice communication and negotiation skills relevant to leadership success.

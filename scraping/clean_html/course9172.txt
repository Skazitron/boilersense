VM 81004 - Conservation Medicine And Exotic Pet Medicine And Surgery In Czech Republic And Austria

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course is a 3-week international rotation for 2nd and 3rd year DVM students in Czech Republic and Austria. Students will spend 4-8 days in UVPS clinics and several days onsite at Zoo Brno. Additional lectures, animal science and behavior experience in both countries. Permission of department required. Typically offered Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Increase understanding of zoo, wildlife and exotic pet medicine in its international context.
2.  Increase awareness of global conservation and health issues.
3.  Develop an understanding of the importance of a global education.
4.  Develop an understanding of the unique issues encountered in exotic pet medicine in central Europe.
5.  Develop an understanding of caring for collections of several large urban zoos.
6.  Increase awareness of international veterinary career opportunities.
7.  Encourage development of leadership skills and diversity awareness.
8.  Foster relationships between Purdue University, UVPS Brno, VetMedUni, as well as zoo colleagues at Zoo Brno, Zoo Praha and the Vienna Zoo.

SFT 35100 - Space Security, Law, Policy, And Doctrine I

Credit Hours: 3.00.  An examination of space law and policy at the national and international levels with particular focus on the Outer Space Treaty of 1967 and the United Nations' Remote Sensing Principles. Students examine how these laws and policies are viewed in real-world situations.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Explain the historical development of space law and policy.
2.  Compare and contrast Space Force doctrine and joint doctrine.
3.  Compare and contrast space law, policy, and doctrine in the historical context of space security.
4.  Describe current U.S. Space policy and law.

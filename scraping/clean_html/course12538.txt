FVS 26100 - Foundations Of Cinema Production

Credit Hours: 3.00.  The course introduces students to the principles and practice of professional video production.  Through lectures, viewing, discussions, and laboratory exercises, students will acquire the tools necessary to transform their concepts, or those of their clients, into successful video productions.  Film students have priority. Typically offered Fall Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Provide an introduction to the cinema production cycle.
2.  Involve the student in preparing materials specific to each stage of the cinema production cycle.
3.  Summarize visual storytelling concepts and production terminology.
4.  Define concepts of cinema production and image capture techniques.
5.  Introduce students to industry specific copyright laws and regulations.
6.  Introduce students to career opportunities including narrative, documentary, television and live event production.

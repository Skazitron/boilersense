VM 40900 - International Veterinary Medicine

Credit Hours: 0.50.  The course will consist of a series of lectures and discussions addressing international aspects of veterinary medicine. The course will include the following topics: foreign animal diseases, biosecurity, animal welfare and ethics, veterinary medicine around the world, food security, and cultural competence. Typically offered Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Become familiar with international issues that impact the veterinary profession.
2.  Develop an understanding of the role that veterinary professionals play in global education and global health.
3.  Develop respect for diverse people and cultures.
4.  Develop an understanding of the skills required for working across cultures.
5.  Develop the knowledge and skills necessary to keep themselves and others safe when traveling internationally and to maximize the benefits of their experience.

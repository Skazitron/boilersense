VM 20800 - Large Animal Medical Nursing Clinical Mentorship -DL

Credit Hours: 1.00.  This clinical mentorship focuses on the principles of basic handling, nursing, and diagnostic techniques used in large animal medicine.  Permission of department required.  Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

    1.000 Credit hours

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Perform handling and restraint techniques on cattle, sheep, goats, and swine.
2.  Perform knot-tying techniques.
3.  Perform assistance with food animal bandaging techniques.
4.  Perform food animal sample collection techniques.
5.  Perform animal injection techniques.
6.  Perform data collection techniques in food animals (TPR, etc.)
7.  Perform techniques for administration of oral medications in the ruminant.
8.  Perform placement and maintenance of indwelling catheters in food animals.
9.  Perform determination of age food animals.
10.  Perform the California Mastitis Test (CMT) technique.

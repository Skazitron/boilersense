ECE 43200 - Elements Of Power System Engineering

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Fundamental concepts of power system analysis, transmission line parameters, basic system models, steady-state performance, network calculations, power flow solutions, fault studies, symmetrical components, operating strategies, and control.

    3.000 Credit hours

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Understand the function of the main components in a power system, and the basis of their circuit models.
2.  Able to build a system representation from components' circuit models and to apply solution techniques to certain operational needs.

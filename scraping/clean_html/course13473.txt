EDPS 31001 - Characteristics Of Individuals With Disability Who Require Intense Intervention

Credit Hours: 3.00.  This course addresses the characteristics of individuals who require intense intervention and how learning, behavioral, social-emotional, and communicative needs impact their lives. This course will also explore the cultural differences, legal and ethical guidelines, family impact, transition to adulthood, and wraparound services. A field experience will provide opportunities to observe and participate in a school setting (early childhood through age 21). Typically offered Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Identify the academic, behavioral, social-emotional, cognitive, communication, and motor/physical characteristics of students requiring intense intervention.
2.  Understand how language, culture, family background, and environment impact the education and development of individuals requiring intense intervention.
3.  Investigate services within the school and community based on the individual target learner and his/her family needs.
4.  Demonstrate ability to assess and address individual academic, social, communication, daily living, and employment goals EC-21.

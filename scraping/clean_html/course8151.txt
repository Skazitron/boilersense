HK 42200 - Basic Concepts In Exercise Program Design

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Introduces basic program design concepts and rationale to design fitness programs for healthy adults emphasizing cardiovascular training, strength training, flexibility training, and balance.  Typically offered Fall Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Design and implement a safe and effective warm up and cool down protocol specific to the individual.
2.  Create and implement well-planned, individualized, and progressive resistance training programs.
3.  Develop progressive and individualized cardiovascular training programs.
4.  Describe the role of exercise in healthy weight management.
5.  Apply the scientific concepts to design exercise programs to improve athletic performance and optimize fitness in non-athlete healthy persons.

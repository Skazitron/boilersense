NUPH 49500 - Applied Nuclear Pharmacy

Credit Hours: 2.00.  This is an elective course that consists of lecture, laboratory, and distance education components which will count as part of the 200 hours of didactic training required for Authorized Nuclear Pharmacist (ANP) status. This course focuses on the preparation, dispensing and clinical applications of radiopharmaceutical products. Typically offered Fall.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Understand the preparation of a variety of radiopharmaceutical products.
2.  Enhance skills in kit selection, product specific compounding requirements, radiochemical purity testing and dispensing of final doses for patient use.
3.  Utilize practical pharmaceutical compounding skills including sterile product preparation guidelines as they apply to radiopharmaceutical preparation.
4.  Identify clinical applications of products being prepared in the nuclear pharmacy laboratory.
5.  Identify and problem solve clinical issues related to various radiopharmaceuticals.

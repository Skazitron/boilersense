NUTR 52300 - Trends In Nutrition & Dietetics

Credit Hours: 2.00.  Analysis and discussion of current trends in dietetics practice and nutrition including such topics as nutrigenomics, informatics, sustainability, agricultural practices, food policies, and global health. Permission of department required.

    2.000 Credit hours


Syllabus Available

Levels: Graduate, Professional, Undergraduate 

Schedule Types: Distance Learning, Lecture 


Offered By: College of Health & Human Sci 

Department: Nutrition Science 


Course Attributes: Upper Division 


May be offered at any of the following campuses:      

      West Lafayette


Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Demonstrate how to locate, interpret, evaluate, and use professional literature to make ethical, evidence-based practice decisions.
2.  Apply critical thinking skills.
3.  Demonstrate effective and professional oral and written communication and documentation.
4.  Assess the impact of a public policy position on nutrition and dietetics practice.
5.  Describe basic concepts of nutritional genomics.

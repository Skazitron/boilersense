HDFS 31400 - Atypical Child Development

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Atypical child development from conception to adolescence can emerge from genetic, medical/health, educational, psychological, and/or contextual factors. This course will survey factors (e.g., premature birth, obesity, neurodevelopmental disorders, learning difficulties, child abuse, and war) that contribute to atypical child development. It will assess how and when typical and atypical developmental trajectories diverge and the roles of measurement and intervention. Typically offered Fall Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Describe at least three theories of atypical child development.
2.  Differentiate typical and atypical child development and describe the roles measurement/assessment may play in this distinction.
3.  Identify biological and contextual factors that may promote resilience or atypical development during the following developmental periods: prenatal, infancy, early to middle childhood, and adolescence.

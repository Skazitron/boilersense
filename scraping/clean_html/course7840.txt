HDFS 49101 - Mentored Research In Human Development And Family Studies I

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Independent study in which student engages in research under the supervision of a faculty member. Involves developing a research proposal, beginning the project, and submitting a summary of the project plan and progress. First semester of a two-semester sequence to fulfill the requirements of the capstone thesis for the Developmental & Family Science major. Permission of instructor required. Typically offered Fall Spring Summer.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Critically and creatively synthesize knowledge and skills gained as a DVFS major, using them in a research project of the student's interest.
2.  Synthesize and apply previously acquired and new knowledge of the HDFS field through in-depth exploration of a topic of interest.
3.  Observe and participate in the research process, including generating a written report of the project.
4.  Work collaboratively with a research mentor and/or research team.
5.  Make decisions using the logic of the scientific method.

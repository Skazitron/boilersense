THTR 33300 - Acting II: Scene Study

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Continued development of the basic acting tools of objectives, actions, justification, personalization; continued vocal and physical work; introduction of script and character analysis; and the introduction of audition techniques. The course will use material from contemporary dramatic literature in the rehearsal process. Typically offered Fall.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Identify, define, describe and demonstrate the basic principles of the craft of acting.
2.  Identify, define, describe and demonstrate the basic terminology of stage technique.
3.  Identify, define and describe the basic principles of script & character analysis.
4.  Read, investigate, interpret and articulate your analysis of specific plays from the canon of 20th and 21st Century realistic dramatic literature.
5.  Develop critical thinking as it applies to your own work, your peers' work and in production work.

CHM 46200 - Intermediate Organic Chemistry

Credit Hours: 3.00.  Theory and application of organic chemistry and reaction mechanisms to organic synthesis and contemporary research topics in closely related areas.  Topics include stereochemistry, reactive organic intermediates, molecular orbital theory, photochemistry, organic materials chemistry, and chemical biology.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Able to use theory to understand/predict experimental observations.
2.  Able to demonstrate an understanding of the physical properties and a molecular understanding of chemical reactivity and materials.
3.  Able to plan and perform chemical transformations.
4.  Able to present a formal oral summary of a topic in organic chemistry and receive feedback.
5.  Able to write a scientific report using the format of the Chemical Society.
6.  Able to practice computer literacy: use available online and database tools, especially in the Organic Chemistry field.

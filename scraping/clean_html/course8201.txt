HK 64000 - Leadership In Athletic Training

Credit Hours: 2.00.  This course is designed to prepare students to embark on paths of personal leadership development. The course will examine leadership theory and research and emphasize the development of leadership and interpersonal skills. Understanding various methods of bringing about change will be provided and researched by learners, and then discussed in our time together. Students in this course will apply foundational business concepts in athletic training through self-assessment case studies and professional experiential exercises. Permission of department required. Prerequisite: HK 63000 with B- or better. Typically offered Spring.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Compare key organizational behavior theories and concepts related to leadership and management.
2.  Evaluate the unique of structures, processes, and interactions in health care organizations as they relate to leadership, management and policy.
3.  Distinguish the different roles and specific challenges for leadership and management in health care.
4.  Differentiate personal leadership orientation, styles and strengths.
5.  Practice, hone, and demonstrate teamwork skills (e.g., leadership, collaboration, communication, etc.) in consulting team assignments and case analyses.
6.  Develop a professional development plan for long term development as a professional.
7.  Evaluate the value model to develop a business case to supply athletic training services.

ECON 50000 - Mathematics For Economists

Credit Hours: 2.00 or 3.00.  This is a Master's level course in mathematics and its application to economics. Students in this class will review and practice the mathematical methods required to solve micro and macroeconomics theoretical models, especially constrained optimization problems. Content covers algebra, univariate and multivariate calculus and constrained optimization methods.

Learning Outcomes: 
1.  Solve problems requiring algebra and (uni-) multivariate calculus.
2.  Apply algebra and calculus to solving (un)constrained optimization problems required by economic theoretical models.
3.  Analyze assumptions embedded in economic theoretical models, e.g., functional forms and parameter values, using algebra and calculus.
